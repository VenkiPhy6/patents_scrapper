La division serologica contempla que Salmonella enterica, una especie de bacteria Salmonella, tiene una variedad de serovares incluyendo Salmonella gallinarum, Salmonella pullorum, Salmonella typhimurium (ST), Salmonella enteritidis (SE), Salmonella typhi, Salmonella choleraesuis (SC), Salmonella derby (SD). De ellos, choleraesuis y derby son patogenos adaptados al ganado porcino, gallinarum y pullorum son patogenos adaptados a las aves de corral, typhimurium y enteritis son patogenos para los seres humanos y los animales y typhi es un patogeno adaptado a los seres humanos, todos los cuales causan la enfermedad en sus respectivas especies, produciendo un dano enorme a los agricultores y consumidores (Informe Zoobises; Reino Unido 2003). Salmonella has an average genomic GC content of 50 to 52%, which is similar to Escherichia coli and Shigella. The genus Salmonella is a pathogenic microorganism that causes infections in cattle, as well as in humans. The serological division contemplates that Salmonella enterica, a species of Salmonella bacteria, has a variety of serovars including Salmonella gallinarum, Salmonella pullorum, Salmonella typhimurium (ST), Salmonella enteritidis (SE), Salmonella typhi, Salmonella choleraesuis (SC), Salmonella derby ( SD). Of these, choleraesuis and derby are pathogens adapted to pigs, gallinarum and pullorum are pathogens adapted to poultry, typhimurium and enteritis are pathogens for humans and animals and typhi is a pathogen adapted to humans, all which cause the disease in their respective species, producing enormous damage to farmers and consumers (Zoobises Report; United Kingdom 2003).

Recently, the application of the HACCP system (risk analysis and control points) has become a mandatory requirement for all slaughterhouses in Korea as of July 1, 2003, due to the high risk of contamination during the course of the manufacture of animal products with Salmonella, which causes direct damage to pigs and meat hygiene and which is found in the digestive tract of pigs (Jae-gil Yeh. Characterization and Counterplan of Salmonellosis in Pigs. Monthly Magazine of Pig Husbandry. 2004). 
 
 
   La fiebre paratifoidea, la enfermedad infecciosa aguda o cronica del tracto digestivo de los cerdos estan causadas por la infeccion por Salmonella, se caracteriza por gastroenteritis y septicemia y principalmente se produce durante el penodo de engorde. En particular, algunas de las bacterias patogenas que causan esta enfermedad pueden causar intoxicacion alimentaria en los seres humanos debido a la ingestion de carne, y por lo tanto, es una enfermedad que tiene gran importancia para la salud publica. Una variedad de tipos de bacteria Salmonella pueden ser patogenos. Paratyphoid fever, the acute or chronic infectious disease of the digestive tract of pigs is caused by Salmonella infection, is characterized by gastroenteritis and septicemia and mainly occurs during the fattening period. In particular, some of the pathogenic bacteria that cause this disease can cause food poisoning in humans due to the ingestion of meat, and therefore, it is a disease that has great importance for public health. A variety of types of Salmonella bacteria can be pathogenic. Among them, Salmonella choleraesuis and Salmonella typhisuis known to cause pig cholera are the main causes of acute Salmonella sepsis. Acute enteritis occurs during the period of fattening, and is accompanied by irregular appetite, severe watery diarrhea, high fever, loss of vitality, neumorna and nervous signs. Skin discoloration can occur in some severe cases. Salmonella typhimurium, Salmonella enteritidis and Salmonella derby are the main causes of chronic enteritis.

Se mezclo 180 pl de cada solucion de pH con 20 pl de una solucion de bacteriofago (1,0 * 1011 UFP/ml) para dar cada solucion de pH una concentracion de 1 M, seguido de incubacion a temperatura ambiente durante 2 horas. La solucion de reaccion se diluyo en serie y 10 pl de cada dilucion se cultivo a 37 °C durante 18 horas por un procedimiento de superposiciones de agar blando para determinar los tftulos de los lisados de fagos (FIG. 6). La FIG. 6 es el resultado del ensayo de resistencia a los acidos en el bacteriofago OCJ11, que muestra el numero de bacteriofagos supervivientes a pH 2,1, 2,5, 3,0, 3,5, 4,0, 5,5, 6,4, 6,9, 7,4, 8,0, 9,0, 9,8 y 11,0. Sin embargo, el bacteriofago OCJ11 mostro actividad reducida a pH 4 y pH 3,5 y perdio por completo su actividad a pH 3,0 o inferior, en comparacion con un control. Como se muestra en la FIG. 6, el bacteriofago no perdio su actividad y se mantuvo estable por debajo de pH 5,5, mientras que perdio su actividad a pH 3,0 o inferior. In order to determine if OCJ11 survives stably in a low pH environment in the stomach of the pig, the OCJ11 was tested to determine its stability over a wide range of pH (pH 2.1, 2.5, 3.0 , 3.5, 4.0, 5.5, 6.4, 6.9, 7.4, 8.2, 9.0, 9.8 and 11.0). Various solutions of pH (sodium acetate buffer (pH 2.1, pH 4.0, pH 5.5 and pH 6.4), sodium citrate buffer (pH 2.5, pH 3.0 and pH) were prepared 3.5), sodium phosphate buffer (pH 6.9 and pH 7.4) and Tris-HCl (pH 8.2, pH 9.0, pH 9.8 and pH 11.0)) at a concentration 0.2 m. 180 pl of each pH solution was mixed with 20 pl of a bacteriophage solution (1.0 * 1011 PFU / ml) to give each pH solution a 1 M concentration, followed by temperature incubation atmosphere for 2 hours. The reaction solution was serially diluted and 10 µl of each dilution was grown at 37 ° C for 18 hours by a soft agar overlay procedure to determine the phage lysate titres (FIG. 6). FIG. 6 is the result of the acid resistance test in the bacteriophage OCJ11, which shows the number of bacteriophages surviving at pH 2.1, 2.5, 3.0, 3.5, 4.0, 5.5, 6 , 4, 6.9, 7.4, 8.0, 9.0, 9.8 and 11.0. As shown in FIG. 6, the bacteriophage did not lose its activity and remained stable below pH 5.5, while it lost its activity at pH 3.0 or lower.
