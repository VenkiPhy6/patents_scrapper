  結核は身体の器官に影響を及ぼし、いくつかの異なる症状が現れるが、初期感染部位は肺である。肺に影響を及ぼす結核は肺結核として知られている。肺結核は結核の最も圧倒的に起こり得る形態である（「結核」（Tuberculosis）、２００５年、８５、ｐ．２２７−２３４）。肺結核を治療するための現在の化学療法レジメンは、４か月間にわたって１次抗結核薬（イソニアジド、リファンピシン、エサンブトールおよび／またはピラジナミド）の同時投与を行い、その後、イソニアジド、リファンピシンおよび／またはエサンブトールで２か月間治療を行うことからなるが、結核の種類によっては、治療期間はさらに延長され９か月から２年の範囲であり得る。この化学療法レジメンは、１日に一度経口投与の形態で行われ、短い血漿内半減期（「薬剤学の国際学術雑誌」（International Journal of Pharmaceutics）、２００４年、２７６、ｐ．４１−４９）と、過剰用量による副作用（「抗菌化学療法の学術雑誌」（Journal of Antimicrobial Chemotherapy）、２００４年、５４、ｐ．７６１−７６６）とに関係する。これらの副作用は望ましくない体内分布特性に起因する。さらに、経口投与薬については、少量の薬物のみが作用部位（即ち、肺）に到達し、数時間のうちになくなる（「結核」（Tuberculosis）、２００５年、８５、ｐ．２２７−２３４）ということが観察されている。上記の問題は、患者の服薬遵守不良に関連し、多剤耐性結核（ＭＤＲＴＢ）の発生につながる。
 
  Tuberculosis affects the body's organs and several different symptoms appear, but the site of initial infection is the lungs. Tuberculosis that affects the lungs is known as pulmonary tuberculosis. Pulmonary tuberculosis is the most prevalent form of tuberculosis (Tuberculosis, 2005, 85, p. 227-234). Current chemotherapeutic regimens for treating pulmonary tuberculosis include co-administration of primary anti-tuberculosis drugs (isoniazid, rifampicin, esambutol and / or pyrazinamide) for 4 months, followed by isoniazid, rifampicin and / or esambutol 2 Depending on the type of tuberculosis, the treatment period can be further extended and can range from 9 months to 2 years. This chemotherapeutic regimen is administered once a day in an oral dosage form with a short plasma half-life ("International Journal of Pharmaceutics, 2004, 276, pp. 41-49). And side effects due to overdose (Journal of Antimicrobial Chemotherapy, 2004, 54, p. 761-766). These side effects are due to undesirable biodistribution characteristics. Furthermore, for orally administered drugs, only a small amount of drug reaches the site of action (ie, lungs) and disappears within a few hours (Tuberculosis, 2005, 85, p. 227-234). It has been observed. The above problems are related to poor patient compliance and lead to the development of multi-drug resistant tuberculosis (MDRTB).
 
 
 
   
  ＭＤＲＴＢは少なくとも２つの最良の抗結核薬（イソニアジドおよびリファンピシン）に対して耐性を有する結核の形態である（「多剤耐性結核ファクトシート」（Multidrug resistant tuberculosis fact sheet）、疾病管理センター、２００８年）。ＭＤＲＴＢはフルオロキノロン、アミカシン、カナマイシンやカプレオマイシンのようなアミノグリコシド、ｐ−アミノサリチル酸およびチオアセタゾンのような２次抗結核薬で治療される（「薬物耐性結核の治療、ファクトシート」（Treatment of drug resistant tuberculosis, fact sheet）、疾病管理センター、２００７年）。２次結核薬は、用量に関連した副作用や、肺における生物学的利用能不良と係わっており、疾病の根絶の障害となっている。
 
  MDRTB is a form of tuberculosis that is resistant to at least two of the best anti-tuberculosis drugs (isoniazid and rifampicin) ("Multidrug resistant tuberculosis fact sheet", Center for Disease Control, 2008) . MDRTB is treated with fluoroquinolones, amikacin, aminoglycosides such as kanamycin and capreomycin, secondary anti-tuberculosis drugs such as p-aminosalicylic acid and thioacetazone ("Treatment of drug resistant" tuberculosis, fact sheet), Center for Disease Control, 2007). Secondary tuberculosis drugs are associated with dose-related side effects and poor bioavailability in the lungs and are an obstacle to eradication of the disease.

  Similar to the problem of pulmonary tuberculosis, the problem of side effects caused by poor bioavailability of drugs and high doses also occurs in the treatment of MRSA and MSSA pneumonia. Nosocomial pneumonia due to MRSA and ventilator-associated pneumonia are associated with high mortality (International Journal of Antimicrobial Agents, 2007, 30, p. 19-24), which is insufficient Depends on the treatment.

  Primary drugs used to treat MRSA pneumonia include vancomycin and linezolid. Vancomycin, a drug selected to treat MRSA pneumonia, is associated with poor pharmacokinetic properties in lung tissue and has a lung concentration that is just 20% plasma concentration (“antibacterial chemotherapy” (Antimicrob. Agents Chemother.), 1999, 37, p. 281-286). In addition, long-term administration of vancomycin has been associated with nephrotoxicity, a dose limiting factor (Clin. Microbiol Infect., 2006, 12, p. 92-95). Linezolid, accepted for MRSA pneumonia therapy, shows good oral bioavailability (600 mg given orally twice a day), but for gastrointestinal side effects, thrombocytopenia and reversible anemia There is a relationship (Clinical Infect. Dis., 2003, 37, pp. 1609-1616). Rarely, the administration of linezolid is also associated with optic neuropathy and peripheral neuropathy ("Antibacterial Chemotherapy Journal" (2004, 53, pp. 1141-1115).

  Beta-lactams (such as ampicillin and cephalosporin) are very effective against MSSA pneumonia as first line therapy. In addition, vancomycin does not relatively penetrate into lung tissue ("US Respiratory Disease", 2006, p. 62-64). In summary, MRSA / MSSA pneumonia therapy has several drawbacks, such as poor pulmonary bioavailability of drugs, toxicity due to drug dosage, and the like.
