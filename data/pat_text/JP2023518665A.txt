  Inflammation is essentially a biological response to harmful irritation or damage to the tissues of an organism, which can be caused by external causes such as contact with irritants or microorganisms. Inflammation is considered the body's protective response aimed at eliminating the source of tissue damage, clearing damaged necrotic tissue from the original injury and the inflammatory process, and initiating tissue repair. This response of the organism involves immune cells and molecular mediators.
 
 
 
   
  現在、炎症は、非ステロイド系又はステロイド系抗炎症薬の全身投与又は局所投与によって治療される。抗炎症剤が広く普及しているにもかかわらず、炎症が抗炎症薬による治療後に持続する可能性があるといういくつかの後遺障害リスクが残っている。さらに、抗炎症治療は、局所適用及び全身投与のいずれの場合においても副作用がないわけではない。
 
  Currently, inflammation is treated by systemic or topical administration of non-steroidal or steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs. Despite the widespread use of anti-inflammatory drugs, some residual risk remains that inflammation may persist after treatment with anti-inflammatory drugs. Furthermore, anti-inflammatory treatments, both topically and systemically applied, are not without side effects.

  In recent years, the incidence of side effects with topical administration has increased due to the abuse or misuse of steroid creams intended to treat skin irritations.

  The supernatant is then removed, leaving the pellet to settle to the bottom of the tube, and the pellet is collected and washed with water. The pellet is then resuspended in physiological (saline) solution to obtain cells and further centrifuged to remove residual culture medium. Cells suspended in water are processed mechanically with a mechanical agitator such as Ultratturrax (mechanical lysis).

 Typically, cell wall disruption by bead milling is performed in a bead mill containing a jacketed grinding chamber with a rotating shaft through its center. The shaft is fitted with an agitator that imparts kinetic energy to the beads in the chamber, causing them to collide with each other (Chisti & Moo-Young, 1986; Middelberg, 1995). Suitable beads may be 0.10-0.15 mm in diameter to effectively destroy bacteria. Large industrial equipment can use beads with a diameter of 0.4-0.6 mm for the mechanism that separates the beads from the suspension (Kula & Shutte, 1987). A suitable tip velocity is at least 10 m  −1  for destruction of bacteria (Kula & Shutte, 1987). Cell concentration can vary from 40-50% wet weight in the broth introduced to the chamber. 
 
 
   
  適切な固体剪断はまた、超音波処理及びＨｕｇｈｅｓプレス又はフレンチプレスを含む高圧法も含み、この方法では、細胞の凍結懸濁液が高圧によって小さな開口部を通って押し出される（Ｅｎｇｌｅｒ，１９８５）。
 
  Suitable solid shear also includes high pressure methods, including sonication and Hughes or French presses, in which a frozen suspension of cells is forced through small orifices by high pressure (Engler, 1985).

  Sonication involves the use of ultrasound, typically sound waves with frequencies above 15-20 kHz, that are capable of disrupting cell walls in suspension. A suitable acoustic power is, for example, when sonicating 5-30 mL of a 20% bacterial suspension in a conventional liquid medium using an acoustic power of 35-95 W.

  Alternatively, as reported by Engler, 1985, mechanical disruption can be obtained with a high pressure valve homogenizer by passing a cell suspension of the strain under high pressure through an adjustable restricted orifice discharge valve. can. Typically, the basic homogenizer design includes a positive displacement pump that forces the cell suspension through the center of the valve seat and across the seating surface. Pressure is controlled by adjusting the force on the valve. Fluid flows radially across the valve and impinges on the impact ring (Middelberg, 1995). Breakdown results from non-specific tearing of the cell wall.

  An exemplary homogenizer type is the Manton-Gaulin APV design (Middelberg, 1995). For example, raise the temperature by about 21 C per 10 MPa in the homogenizer. The method of disruption in homogenizers is strongly influenced by operating pressure. By operating the homogenizer at higher pressures, it is possible to reduce the number of passes of the cell slurry through the homogenizer for a given degree of disruption (Chisti & Moo-Young, 1986; Bury et al., 2001). ).

  A microfluider homogenizer can also be used as an instrument to obtain fragmented cell walls. In this device, two streams of cell suspension are impacted against a stationary surface at high velocity and the energy input is dissipated almost instantaneously at the point of impact, resulting in cell disruption (Middelberg, 1995; Agerkvist & Enfors, 1990). . The residence time of the strain suspension in the microfluidizer disruption chamber, the hottest part of the device, is 25-40 ms. Cooling in place can be achieved by immersing the disruption chamber in an ice bath (Sauer et al., 1989; Geciova, personal experience). The percentage of broken cells increases with increasing pressure and number of passes.

  ハチノスツヅリガ（Ｇ．ｍｅｌｌｏｎｅｌｌａ）の幼虫は、局所適用、経口送達、及び注射など、病原体の容易な送達のための複数の選択肢を与える。微生物は、幼虫のヘモコールに直接注射することができ、したがって、幼虫は既知の量の病原体を受け取る。
 
  G. mellonella larvae offer multiple options for facile delivery of pathogens, including topical application, oral delivery, and injection. Microorganisms can be injected directly into the larval hemocorr, and the larvae thus receive a known amount of the pathogen.

  Another advantage of the G. mellonella model is the possibility to assess the expression of genes related to immunity and stress. Thereby, to examine the effect of the design on G. mellonella larvae inoculated with S. aureus supernatant, we investigated the immunity and stress genes of G. mellonella.   結論 
  黄色ブドウ球菌（Ｓｔａｐｈｙｌｏｃｏｃｃｕｓ  ａｕｒｅｕｓ）の病原性株による感染は、異化物を産生するこれらの微生物が炎症性サイトカインの産生を刺激し、表皮バリアの損傷及び疾患の特徴的な症状の発現の一因となるので、アトピー性皮膚炎における有害因子と考えられている。
 CONCLUSIONS Infection with virulent strains of Staphylococcus aureus stimulates the production of inflammatory cytokines by these catabolite-producing organisms, contributing to epidermal barrier damage and the development of characteristic symptoms of the disease. Therefore, it is considered a detrimental factor in atopic dermatitis. 
 
 
   
  得られた結果は、ＬｉｍｐｉＡＤ成分が、注射されたハチノスツヅリガ（Ｇ．ｍｅｌｌｏｎｅｌｌａ）幼虫の生存に対する黄色ブドウ球菌（Ｓ．ａｕｒｅｕｓ）によって産生される異化物の影響を低減することを示唆している。
 
  The results obtained suggest that the LimpiAD component reduces the impact of catabolites produced by S. aureus on the survival of injected G. mellonella larvae.
 
 
 
   
  さらに、ＬｉｍｐｉＡＤによる処置は、炎症促進性サイトカインの発現を減少させ、細胞接着及び遊走に関与する１８－ｗｅａｌｅｒ遺伝子の発現を増加させた。代謝ストレス及び免疫遺伝子の発現から得られたデータは、ＬｉｍｐｉＡＤ考案物の作用機序に関するさらなる証拠を提供する。
 
  Furthermore, treatment with LimpiAD decreased the expression of pro-inflammatory cytokines and increased the expression of 18-wealer genes involved in cell adhesion and migration. Data from metabolic stress and immune gene expression provide further evidence for the mechanism of action of the LimpiAD design.

  The bacterial pellet was first subjected to a degreasing procedure followed by a Soxhlet treatment using organic solvents (i.e. ether-ethanol, chloroform, methanol-chloroform, mixtures thereof) solvents, and then it was dried under a hood laminar flow. . After drying, the pellets were homogenized by a two-step Ultratturrax treatment (20 seconds to 10 minutes each) with the addition of distilled water (at a ratio of 1:2 p/V). After centrifugation, the supernatant was warmed to 80°C, then chilled in cold water, preferably 3-15°C, and finally chilled on ice. A subsequent fragment precipitation step was performed by incubation with 15-40% v/v cold ammonium sulfate for 24 hours at 4°C. After incubation, the suspension was centrifuged and the precipitated fragments were collected and lyophilized.
