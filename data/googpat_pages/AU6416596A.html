WO 97/03091 PCT/EP96/02782 1 Novel process for the preparation of peptides and N-carbamoyl-protected peptides The invention relates to a process for the enzymatic 5 preparation of protected di- or oligopeptides and removal of the protective group used by splitting off. Synthetic short-chain peptides are increasingly used in pharmacology and in parenteral feeding. Kyotorphin (L-Tyr 10 L-Arg), which promotes the release of enkephalins, that is to say endogenous substances which have an analgesic and tranquillizing action in the brain (Hughes, 1975), may be mentioned as an example of a pharmacologically active dipeptide. 15 The enzymatic preparation of di- or oligopeptides is already known and generally makes use of protective group technology. The use of a formyl protective group is thus described in US-A-571,037, that of an acetyl protective 20 group in JP 62074296, that of a benzyl protective group in US-A-4,935,355 or that of a phenacetyl protective group in Tetrahedron 1992, 48, 1115. These variants have the disadvantage that the protective groups in some cases are not inexpensive (benzyl, phenacetyl), can be split off 25 again only with difficulty (acetyl, formyl) or can be removed (benzyl) only under quite specific conditions (hydrogenolysis). The object of the invention was therefore to develop a 30 process for the synthesis of peptides which is particularly simple and inexpensive, allows simple and gentle removal of the protective group by splitting off, and allows simple working up and separating-off of the enzyme.
