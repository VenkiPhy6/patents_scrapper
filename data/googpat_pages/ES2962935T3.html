En el campo de la agricultura se conocen muchos fertilizantes. En particular, se usan comnmente fertilizantes a base de nitrgeno, tales como amoniaco, nitrato de amonio (NH4NO3) y urea, para mantener el rendimiento del cultivo lo ms alto posible. Many fertilizers are known in the field of agriculture. In particular, nitrogen-based fertilizers, such as ammonia, ammonium nitrate (NH4NO3), and urea, are commonly used to keep crop yields as high as possible. In fact, many biological processes in plants involve nitrogen; For example, nitrogen is involved in the production of amino acids and therefore proteins.

Sin embargo, solo se convierte en materia vegetal una fraccin de los fertilizantes a base de nitrgeno que se aplican a los cultivos. La cantidad de fertilizante que no se convierte en materia vegetal puede acumularse en el suelo o puede fluir hacia las aguas superficiales o hacia el agua subterrnea, causando con ello la contaminacin del agua. However, only a fraction of the nitrogen-based fertilizers applied to crops are converted into plant matter. The amount of fertilizer that is not converted into plant matter can accumulate in the soil or can flow into surface water or groundwater, thereby causing water pollution.

Para reducir el uso de fertilizantes qumicos, pueden usarse inoculantes que incluyen bacterias fijadoras del nitrgeno para remplazar parcialmente tales fertilizantes qumicos. To reduce the use of chemical fertilizers, inoculants that include nitrogen-fixing bacteria can be used to partially replace such chemical fertilizers.

The main reservoirs of methylotrophic bacteria are generally soil and water (both fresh and salt), but these bacteria are also present in a wide variety of natural and man-made environments, including dust, lake sediments, air, facial creams, fermented products, water supply networks, bathrooms and air conditioning systems.

Sin embargo, los inoculantes conocidos no permiten resultados satisfactorios en el estmulo del crecimiento de las plantas y tampoco permiten la sustitucin de los fertilizantes qumicos, ni una reduccin significativa en el uso de tales productos qumicos. Resumen del experimento Experiment Summary

El objetivo de este experimento era evaluar los efectos de la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin (3 x 107 UFC/g) sobre el rendimiento del cultivo de escarola cuando se reduce la dosis de nitrgeno (fertilizacin). El experimento se realiz en la zona noreste de Espaa, en el municipio de Vila-Sacra (Girona), a campo abierto, en una pequea zona tpica para el cultivo de verduras. El estudio se efectu respetando los principios de Buenas Prcticas Experimentales definidos por la Normativa 1107/2009 de la Unin Europea. The objective of this experiment was to evaluate the effects of the new strain of Methylobacteriumsp. of the invention (3 x 107 CFU/g) on the yield of the endive crop when the nitrogen dose is reduced (fertilization). The experiment was carried out in the northeast of Spain, in the municipality of Vila-Sacra (Girona), in open fields, in a small area typical for growing vegetables. The study was carried out respecting the principles of Good Experimental Practices defined by Regulation 1107/2009 of the European Union.

La cepa de la invencin se prob a la misma dosis (300 g de f.p./ha) en tres zonas con diferentes dosis de fertilizacin nitrogenada (100% (habitual), 60% y 40%), y se evalu y compar con un control no tratado (con el 100% de fertilizacin nitrogenada, pero sin la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin). The strain of the invention was tested at the same dose (300 g f.p./ha) in three areas with different doses of nitrogen fertilization (100% (usual), 60% and 40%), and was evaluated and compared with a control untreated (with 100% nitrogen fertilization, but without the new strain of Methylobacterium sp. of the invention).

La fertilizacin se efectu antes del trasplante (23 de agosto de 2018). El producto fue aplicado en la fase 17-19 de BBCH (28 de septiembre de 2018), usando una mochila pulverizadora calibrada HONDA WRJ2525. Fertilization was carried out before transplanting (August 23, 2018). The product was applied in BBCH phase 17-19 (September 28, 2018), using a calibrated HONDA WRJ2525 backpack sprayer.

Efectos del producto Product effects

Segn las condiciones del experimento, puede concluirse que la aplicacin de la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin con una dosis reducida de nitrgeno obtuvo un aumento numrico del peso medio de la planta (kg) y una produccin comercial (nmero de plantas por hectrea) en comparacin con una nica aplicacin de fertilizante convencional (sin la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin), lo que quiere decir que la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin aport el nitrgeno necesario para un correcto desarrollo de la planta. According to the conditions of the experiment, it can be concluded that the application of the new strain of Methylobacteriumsp. of the invention with a reduced dose of nitrogen obtained a numerical increase in the average plant weight (kg) and commercial production (number of plants per hectare) compared to a single application of conventional fertilizer (without the new strain of Methylobacteriumsp. de the invention), which means that the new strain of Methylobacterium sp. of the invention provided the nitrogen necessary for proper development of the plant.

Los tratamientos 3 y 4 lograron un aumento en el rendimiento de la cosecha del 3,8 y el 13,89%, respectivamente, con respecto al tratamiento 1, obteniendo diferencias estadsticas significativas. No se obtuvo ninguna diferencia significativa entre tratamientos con la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin tratamientos con dosis diferentes de nitrgeno en el resto de los parmetros valorados, pese a que los resultados obtenidos sugeran resultados ptimos con una mayor reduccin del nitrgeno. Treatments 3 and 4 achieved an increase in crop yield of 3.8 and 13.89%, respectively, with respect to treatment 1, obtaining significant statistical differences. No significant difference was obtained between treatments with the new strain of Methylobacterium sp. of the invention treatments with different doses of nitrogen in the rest of the parameters evaluated, despite the fact that the results obtained suggested optimal results with a greater reduction in nitrogen.

La nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin se aplic con una dosis de 300 g/ha (3 * 107 UFC/g) cuando las plantas del cultivo tenan entre 7 y 9 hojas (BBCH 17-19). The new strain of Methylobacteriumsp. of the invention was applied with a dose of 300 g/ha (3 * 107 CFU/g) when the crop plants had between 7 and 9 leaves (BBCH 17-19).

La cepa de la invencin aplicada a razn de 300 g/ha (3 * 107 UFC/g) con fertilizacin convencional (N = 40%) present los mejores resultados del experimento, con un aumento del 13,89% en el rendimiento, con diferencia estadstica significativa. The strain of the invention applied at a rate of 300 g/ha (3 * 107 CFU/g) with conventional fertilization (N = 40%) presented the best results of the experiment, with an increase of 13.89% in yield, with significant statistical difference.

En cuanto a los dems parmetros valorados, no hubo diferencias estadsticas significativas entre los tratamientos, lo que indica que la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin fue capaz de aportar el nitrgeno necesario para el correcto desarrollo de las plantas. Regarding the other parameters evaluated, there were no significant statistical differences between the treatments, which indicates that the new strain of Methylobacterium sp. of the invention was capable of providing the nitrogen necessary for the correct development of plants.

El uso de la nueva cepa deMethylobacteriumsp. de la invencin reduce la cantidad de fertilizacin nitrogenada requerida en los cultivos, dado que los resultados de los parmetros evaluados fueron similares o aun superiores a los obtenidos con fertilizacin convencional. The use of the new strain of Methylobacterium sp. of the invention reduces the amount of nitrogen fertilization required in crops, given that the results of the evaluated parameters were similar or even higher than those obtained with conventional fertilization.
