Baking yeast strain cect10868 and baking strain cect10869; method for the production thereof by means of recombinant dna techniques and their application as baking yeasts

Abstract
translated from

The present invention relates to the production of an industrial strain of baking yeast capable of expressing and exporting to the external cellular medium the endoxylanase enzyme X24 of Aspergillus nidulans and to the production of an industrial baking strain capable of expressing and exporting to the external cellular medium, in combination, the endoxylanase enzyme X24 of Aspergillus nidulans and the α-amylase enzyme of Aspergillus oryzae, in order to enhance the process of degradation of the hemicelluloses and starch present in flour and, consequently, improve the volume, texture and average life of the bread baked. To this effect, the genes corresponding to said enzymes have been introduced into the yeast and their expression has been regulated by a typical yeast promoter, for example that of the gene of actine pACT1 which is, additionally, functional in industrial growth conditions in molasses. Application in genetic engineering techniques and baking yeast.

5.- Producción de endoxilanasa X24 y α-amilasa por los transformantes5.- Production of endoxylanase X24 and α-amylase by transformants
Los transformantes obtenidos se crecieron en medio de cultivo industrial a base de melazas de remolacha, siguiéndose el aumento de biomasa (absorbancia a 600 n ) y la aparición de las actividades endoxilanasa y α-amilasa en el medio de cultivo. La determinación de la actividad xilanásica se llevo a cabo midiendo la liberación de azúcares reductores resultantes de la acción de la enzima sobre xilano de avena. Los azúcares reductores se determinaron por el método del dinitrosalicílico (DNS) [Bailey y col. (1992), Journal Biotechnology 23:257-270]; una unidad se define como la cantidad de enzima que es capaz de liberar 1 μmol de azúcares reductores por minuto a 50°C en las condiciones del ensayo. La determinación de la actividad amilásica se llevo a cabo utilizando el método ceralpha, que usa pnitrofemlmal-toheptaosido como sustrato [Sheenan, H. y McCleary, B.V. (1988), Biotechnology Techniques 2:289-292]; una unidad ceralpha se define como la' cantidad de enzima que es capaz de liberar 1 μmol de p-nitrofenol por minuto a 42°C, en las condiciones del ensayo.The transformants obtained were grown in industrial culture medium based on beet molasses, following the increase in biomass (absorbance at 600 n) and the appearance of endoxylanase and α-amylase activities in the culture medium. The production of these two enzymes by the transformants tested [YEpACT-X2], [YEpACT-AMY] and [YEpACT-AMY-ACT-X24] is shown in Figure 3. The determination of xylanic activity was carried out by measuring the release of reducing sugars resulting from the action of the enzyme on oats xylan. Reducing sugars were determined by the dinitrosalicylic (DNS) method [Bailey et al. (1992), Journal Biotechnology 23: 257-270]; One unit is defined as the amount of enzyme that is capable of releasing 1 μmol of reducing sugars per minute at 50 ° C under the test conditions. The determination of amylastic activity was carried out using the ceralpha method, which uses pnitrofemlmal-toheptaoside as a substrate. [Sheenan, H. and McCleary, BV (1988), Biotechnology Techniques 2: 289-292]; A ceralpha unit is defined as the amount of enzyme that is capable of releasing 1 µmol of p-nitrophenol per minute at 42 ° C, under the conditions of the assay.
El transformante [YEpACT-AMY-ACT-X24] fue capaz de secretar cantidades de α-amilasa similares a las del transformante [YEpACT-AMY] , pero los niveles de endoxilanasa X24 fueron inferiores a los del transformante [YEpACT-X24] (Figura 3) .The [YEpACT-AMY-ACT-X24] transformant was able to secrete amounts of α-amylase similar to those of the [YEpACT-AMY] transformant, but the X24 endoxylase levels were lower than those of the [YEpACT-X24] transformant (Figure 3) .

Application in the production of bread and bakery products of industrial yeasts according to claims 1 and 2, characterized in that it is performed by the inoculation of recombinant CECT10868 and / or CECT10869 strains expressing the X24 endoxylanase protein of Aspergill us nidulans , alone or in combination with the α (1-4) amylase protein of Aspergill us oryzae, or by the inoculation of other recombinant strains that express enzymes capable of degrading hemicelluloses and / or starch, preferably endoxylanases and αamylases, so as to contribute to improve the volume, texture and half-life of bread or other bakery products.
