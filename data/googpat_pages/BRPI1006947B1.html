ANTECEDENTESBACKGROUND

A presente inveno refere-se ao Mycoplasma gallisepticum (MG), que um patgeno respiratrio infeccioso de frangos e perus. o patgeno de micoplasma mais patognico e economicamente significativo de aves domsticas. As perdas econmicas por condenao ou desclassificao de carcaas, reduzida eficincia da produo de rao e ovos, e aumento dos custos de medicao so fatores que tornam isto um dos problemas de doena mais caros confrontando a produo comercial de aves domsticas mundialmente. O controle do MG geralmente por isolamento e manuteno de estoque de criao livre de MG. No entanto, a rpida expanso da produo de aves domsticas em pequenas reas geogrficas e fazendas de mltiplas idades que nunca despovoam tornam a erradicao e o controle de MG por semelhantes esforos de biossegurana isoladamente difceis e necessitam da implementao de medidas adicionais. Nestas situaes, a imunizao profiltica de ave domstica contra doena relacionada com MG envolve o uso de vacinas inativadas ou exposio a cepas de vacinas vivas atenuadas de MG. Vide, por exemplo, Kleven et al., 1997, Acta Vet Hung; 45(3): 299-305.The present invention relates to Mycoplasma gallisepticum (MG), which is an infectious respiratory pathogen in chickens and turkeys. It is the most pathogenic and economically significant mycoplasma pathogen in poultry. Economic losses due to condemnation or declassification of carcasses, reduced efficiency in the production of feed and eggs, and increased medication costs are factors that make this one of the most expensive disease problems facing commercial poultry production worldwide. The control of the MG is usually by isolation and maintenance of stock of free creation of MG. However, the rapid expansion of poultry production in small geographic areas and multi-age farms that never depopulate makes MG eradication and control by similar biosafety efforts in isolation difficult and requires the implementation of additional measures. In these situations, prophylactic immunization of poultry against MG-related disease involves the use of inactivated vaccines or exposure to live attenuated MG vaccine strains. See, for example, Kleven et al., 1997, Acta Vet Hung; 45 (3): 299-305.

No entanto, cada abordagem tem desvantagens. As vacinas inativadas, apesar de geralmente eficazes na proteo contra perda de produo de ovos em galinhas poedeiras (layers), no previnem infeco de modo confivel ou proporcionam proteo consistente contra doena respiratria. E, apesar das vacinas vivas atenuadas de MG parecerem ser mais eficazes, e portanto mais populares, do que vacinas inativadas, elas podem produzir doena ou conferir funo reprodutiva.However, each approach has disadvantages. Inactivated vaccines, although generally effective in protecting against loss of egg production in laying hens (layers), do not reliably prevent infection or provide consistent protection against respiratory disease. And although live attenuated MG vaccines appear to be more effective, and therefore more popular, than inactivated vaccines, they can produce disease or confer reproductive function.

Uma caracterstica importante de uma vacina viva de MG eficaz a capacidade para aumentar a resistncia a infeco por cepa selvagem, e para substituir cepas selvagens com a cepa de vacina sobre stios de produo de mltiplas idades (Levisohn e Kleven, 2000, Rev Sci Tech; 19(2): 42542; e Turner e Kleven, 1998, Avian Dis; 42(2): 404-7). Atualmente, vacinas vivas disponveis para o controle de MG incluem cepa F (Luginbuhl et al., 1967, Ann NY Acad Sci; 143: 234-238; Adler et al., 1960, Am J Vet Res; 21: 482-485), 6/85 (Evans e Hafez, 1992, Avian Dis; 36: 197-201) e ts-11 (Whithear et al., 1990, Aust Vet J; Q7: 159-165; e Whithear et al., 1990, Aust Vet J; 67: 168-174). A cepa F transmissvel para companheiros de galinheiro e frangos no vacinados em galinheiros adjacentes e pode ser isolada de granjas muito depois de a vacinao ter cessado. As cepas ts-11 e 6/85 so transmissveis, embora insuficientemente, de aves domsticas vacinadas para no vacinadas quando em contato. A cepa F persiste em nveis mais elevados no trato respiratrio superior do que quer ts-11 ou 6/85, e ts-11 parece colonizar de modo mais eficaz do que 6/85. A cepa F tambm transmite da galinha para o ovo. Infelizmente, embora cada uma das vacinas disponveis atualmente tenha suas vantagens, nenhuma delas atinge o status ideal em todos os aspectos. Portanto, existe, por conseguinte, a necessidade de cepas de vacinas vivas de MG aprimoradas que sejam tanto seguras quanto eficazes, que sejam estveis e no virulentas.An important feature of an effective live MG vaccine is the ability to increase resistance to infection by wild strain, and to replace wild strains with the vaccine strain on sites of multiple age production (Levisohn and Kleven, 2000, Rev Sci Tech ; 19 (2): 42542; and Turner and Kleven, 1998, Avian Dis; 42 (2): 404-7). Currently, live vaccines available for MG control include strain F (Luginbuhl et al., 1967, Ann NY Acad Sci; 143: 234-238; Adler et al., 1960, Am J Vet Res; 21: 482-485) , 6/85 (Evans and Hafez, 1992, Avian Dis; 36: 197-201) and ts-11 (Whithear et al., 1990, Aust Vet J; Q7: 159-165; and Whithear et al., 1990, Aust Vet J; 67: 168-174). Strain F is transmissible to chicken coops and unvaccinated chickens in adjacent chicken coops and can be isolated from farms long after vaccination has ceased. The strains ts-11 and 6/85 are transmissible, although insufficiently, from vaccinated to non-vaccinated poultry when in contact. Strain F persists at higher levels in the upper respiratory tract than either ts-11 or 6/85, and ts-11 appears to colonize more effectively than 6/85. The F strain also transmits from the chicken to the egg. Unfortunately, although each of the vaccines currently available has its advantages, none of them reach ideal status in all aspects. Therefore, there is, therefore, a need for enhanced MG live vaccine strains that are both safe and effective, that are stable and non-virulent.
