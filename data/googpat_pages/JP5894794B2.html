  The present invention relates to a method for transforming stramenopile. Further, the present invention relates to a stramenopile having an unsaturated fatty acid content increased by introducing a fatty acid desaturase gene, a method for producing an unsaturated fatty acid from these unsaturated fatty acid content-increased stramenopile, and the like.

poly unsaturated fatty acid, PUFAEPADHA3 n-3   Polyunsaturated fatty acid (PUFA) is an important component in animal and human nutrition. 3 polyunsaturated fatty acids (also known as n-3 polyunsaturated fatty acids), such as eicosapentaenoic acid (EPA) or docosahexaenoic acid (DHA), are used in pediatric brain development, eye function, hormones and other signaling substances. Because they play a variety of roles in health aspects including synthesis and prevention of cardiovascular disorders, cancer and diabetes (Non-Patent Document 1), they are important ingredients in human nutrition. For that reason, the production of highly unsaturated fatty acids is needed.

  On the other hand, microorganisms classified as Labyrinthula are already known to produce highly unsaturated fatty acids. Among microorganisms belonging to the genus Azolidaceae, a method for producing highly unsaturated fatty acid-containing phospholipids using a microorganism belonging to the genus Schizochytrium (Patent Document 1), a microorganism belonging to the genus Thraustochytrium having an ability to produce docosahexaenoic acid (Patent Document 2), etc. Has already been reported. There is a great need for a convenient and economical process for the production of these unsaturated fatty acids, particularly in eukaryotic systems, in order to enable fortification of food and / or feed.

SchizochytriumAuranthiochytriumPKS   In Labyrinthula, a method for introducing a foreign gene has already been reported for a specific strain belonging to the genus Schizophytrium (in the current classification system (Non-patent Document 2), belonging to the genus Auranthiochytrium (Non-patent Document 3)) ( Patent Documents 3 and 4). Further, as a method for causing a change in fatty acid composition by transformation, an example is known in which a polyketide synthase (PKS) gene is disrupted and as a result the fatty acid composition is changed (Non-patent Document 4). To date, there have been no reports of manipulating enzymes constituting the desaturase pathway to change fatty acid composition.
