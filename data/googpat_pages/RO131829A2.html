Sunt cunoscute diferite tipuri de biopreparate destinate tratamentului resturilor vegetale n sistemele de agricultur conservativ. Aceste sisteme de agricultur conservativ se caracterizeaz nu numai prin reducerea lucrrilor solului, ci i prin meninerea solului acoperit cu resturi vegetale. In timpul vegetaiei culturilor principale acoperirea este asigurat de resturile vegetale rezultate din culturile premergtoare, care sunt lsate fixate sau tocate dup recoltare, i/sau dintr-o cultur de acoperire n timpul iernii, transformat n muici nainte de nsmnarea culturii principale, i/sau prin mulcirea cu materiale vegetale (Hobbs et al. 2008, Philosophical Transactions of the Poyal Society B: Biological Sciences, 363:543-555.). Resturile vegetale care acoper solul limiteaz evaporarea apei, faciliteaz infiltrarea precipitaiilor, reduc eroziune, amelioreaz structura solului, cresc coninutul de materie organic i de carbon, i modereaz temperatura solului n zonele calde (Scopel et al. 2013, Agronomy for Sustainable Development, 33:113-130).There are known different types of biopreparations intended for the treatment of plant residues in conservative agriculture systems. These systems of conservative agriculture are characterized not only by reducing soil work, but also by maintaining soil covered with plant debris. During the vegetation of the main crops, the cover is provided by the vegetal debris resulting from the pre-harvest crops, which are left fixed or chopped after harvesting, and / or from a cover crop during the winter, transformed into mosses before sowing the main crop, and / or by mulching with plant materials (Hobbs et al. 2008, Philosophical Transactions of the Poyal Society B: Biological Sciences, 363: 543-555.). Vegetable debris covering the soil limits water evaporation, facilitates precipitation infiltration, reduces erosion, improves soil structure, increases organic matter and carbon content, and moderates soil temperature in hot areas (Scopel et al. 2013, Agronomy for Sustainable Development, 33: 113-130).

In afara numeroaselor avantaje sunt i o serie de efecte negative asociate sistemelor cu nivel ridicat de resturi vegetale pe sol. Resturile de plante favorizeaz dezvoltarea agenilor fitopatogeni din sol (Bockus i Shroyer, 1998, Annual Review of Phytopathology, 36:, 485-500), inclusiv a celor devastani, cum sunt de exemplu cei care produc fuzarioza spicului (Guo et al. 2010, Plant Pathology, 59: 1107-1113). Nivelele ridicate de resturi vegetale pot reduce disponibilitatea azotului pentru culturile principale n stadiile iniiale de dezvoltare (Geisseler et al. 2010, Soil Biology and Biochemistry, 42:2058-2067). Resturile vegetale au efecte nefavorabile asupra nfiinrii culturii i dezvoltrii ei n primele faze de vegetaie datorit reducerii temperaturii solului (Morris et al. 2009. European Journal of Agronomy, 30: 151-162).Apart from the numerous advantages, there are also a number of negative effects associated with systems with high levels of plant debris on the soil. Plant remains favor the development of phytopathogenic agents in the soil (Bockus and Shroyer, 1998, Annual Review of Phytopathology, 36:, 485-500), including the devastating ones, such as those producing sputum fusosa (Guo et al. 2010, Plant Pathology, 59: 1107-1113). High levels of plant debris can reduce the availability of nitrogen for the main crops in the early stages of development (Geisseler et al. 2010, Soil Biology and Biochemistry, 42: 2058-2067). Vegetable debris has adverse effects on the establishment of the crop and its development in the early stages of vegetation due to the reduction of soil temperature (Morris et al. 2009. European Journal of Agronomy, 30: 151-162).
