  The present invention relates to a synergistic combination of a lipopeptide with one of several compounds. The present invention further provides a method for controlling fungal and bacterial diseases using a synergistic composition.

  Fungicides include uses for protecting crops, as preservatives for food, feed and cosmetics, and as therapeutics for human and veterinary applications, and the like. There are countless uses. Low crop yields, foodborne diseases and fungal infections in both humans and animals are problems in both developed and developing countries.

  Non-ribosomal peptides, including amphipathic cyclic lipopeptides such as surfactins, itrins and fenginins, are well recognized for their antimicrobial properties and have been used in the field of crop protection. They also have potential utility in biopharmaceuticals and other biotechnological applications because of their mechanism of action. Lipopeptides can be obtained by fermentation of various soil bacteria, such as Bacillus subtilis and Bacillus amyloliquefaciens. Lipopeptides kill fungi by disrupting cell membranes. Since these compounds act directly on membrane lipids and do not act on single-site protein targets, the likelihood of fungal resistance developing these compounds is extremely low It is expected. In addition, lipopeptides have a low risk to workers and consumers; indeed, crops treated with products based on Bacillus can be harvested on the day of processing.

  The lipopeptides produced by Bacillus subtilis and Bacillus amyloliquefaciens are limited when applied to plants, plant parts or soil due to their hydrophobicity. Can only have high bioavailability. Compounds that increase the bioavailability of these lipopeptides could enhance their antimicrobial activity. As shown in Examples 28 and 29 below, lipopeptides are enhanced when their ability to disperse in aqueous supernatants is enhanced rather than their ability to remain in the cell pellet. (See FIGS. 5 and 6).

  There is a need for improved formulations having improved antimicrobial activity of lipopeptide producing strains of Bacillus subtilis and Bacillus amyloliquefaciens. It would be highly desirable to improve the efficacy of products based on Bacillus, especially products based on Bacillus where fungal resistance is less likely to develop. There is a continuing need in the art for such improvements in bactericidal formulations based on Bacillus.

  The present invention relates to the enhancement of a lipopeptide-producing strain of Bacillus subtilis or Bacillus amyloliquefaciens and one or more of several compounds combined in a synergistically effective amount. Related formulations. The invention further provides compositions having synergistic antibacterial activity. Also provided are novel antimicrobial compositions and methods of using those compositions.
