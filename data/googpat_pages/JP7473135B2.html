The present invention relates to a sweet potato plant that is rich in a pelargonidin-based anthocyanin compound having a hydroxycinnamic acid modification group as a pigment component. The present invention also relates to a sweet potato-derived anthocyanin pigment composition that has bright red coloring and compound stability.


Anthocyanin pigments are pigment compositions that are an aggregate of multiple similar compounds, and the compound composition varies greatly depending on the type and variety of the raw plant, the growing period, the growing environment, etc. In addition to the combination of anthocyanidin, which is an aglycone, and sugar molecules, an enormous number of combinations of similar compounds exist depending on the presence and type of organic acids and various modifying groups, and each has different molecular properties related to color tone, stability, etc.
Sweet potato pigment is a type of anthocyanin pigment mainly produced from sweet potato roots, and has a relatively good odor characteristic derived from the raw material, making it a pigment material that does not require excessive deodorization during the refining process. In addition, since sweet potato pigment is a natural pigment derived from plant raw materials, it overlaps with the recent trend of consumers toward natural products, and is used as an excellent pigment material in many fields such as food and beverages, cosmetics, quasi-drugs, and pharmaceuticals.



Ordinary sweet potato-derived anthocyanin pigments have a composition mainly composed of cyanidin- and peonidin-based anthocyanins in terms of the type of aglycone, and their coloring characteristics reflect the compound composition and exhibit a purple to reddish purple hue in the weak acidic range, so that if a bright and vivid red anthocyanin pigment is desired, ordinary sweet potato pigments cannot be used.
Here, when an anthocyanin pigment with a bright and vivid red tone is desired, the sweet potato pigment with the above-mentioned reddish purple tone is unsuitable, and the red radish pigment is considered to be suitable in terms of its coloring characteristics. However, the red radish pigment has essential problems regarding odor characteristics and back-odor due to the characteristics of its glucosinolate composition.
In addition, a general issue with anthocyanin pigments is the stable maintenance of the coloring properties of the pigment compounds, and sweet potato pigments and red radish pigments are recognized as pigment compositions with relatively excellent stability because they have compositional characteristics that include a large amount of acylated anthocyanins. However, in the field of food and beverages, which assumes exposure to light during product distribution, there are cases where the stability of ordinary sweet potato pigments and red radish pigments is not sufficient, and there is a demand for the development of anthocyanin pigments with even better light resistance.

The present invention has been made in consideration of the above-mentioned problems in the prior art, and aims to provide an anthocyanin pigment composition derived from sweet potato, which has color-developing properties that result in a vivid red hue and has excellent compound stability.



In light of the above-described state of the prior art, the inventors conducted intensive research and discovered that when they crossed two specific breeding lines created by the inventors as original varieties, sweet potato plants emerged from the cross population and progeny population that had high contents of pelargonidin-based anthocyanin compounds as their main pigment component, which are not found in plants of normal sweet potato varieties.
Furthermore, since the sweet potato plant had a high content of anthocyanin compounds in which specific sites on the sugar chain were acylated with hydroxycinnamic acids, it was determined that the sweet potato plant had an anthocyanin pigment composition that was highly stable and had a bright red color.
Therefore, the present inventors prepared an anthocyanin pigment composition from the tuberous root of the sweet potato plant, and it was shown that the pigment composition exhibited color development characteristics at the maximum absorption wavelength equivalent to that of red radish pigments, and a sweet potato-derived pigment composition exhibiting a vivid red tone could be prepared. In addition, when a light resistance test was performed by light irradiation, it was confirmed that the pigment had significantly superior photostability properties compared to general sweet potato pigments and red radish pigments. In addition, it was shown that the odor characteristics were as good as those of ordinary sweet potato pigments.



Here, the progeny line groups derived from the parent lines "Kyukei 89360-8" and "Kyukei 294" are not lines containing pelargonidin-based anthocyanins, but rather lines that contain cyanidin-based anthocyanins and peonidin-based anthocyanins as pigment components, similar to normal sweet potato lines. Surprisingly, from the cross population obtained by crossing "Kyukei 89360-8" and "Kyukei 294", sweet potato plants that accumulated high amounts of pelargonidin-based anthocyanins appeared at a certain frequency.
Although the details of the mechanism of action of this production mechanism are unknown, it is speculated that in the crossbreeding population of "Kyukei 89360-8" and "Kyukei 294", sweet potato plants that have acquired metabolic functions that enable the production and accumulation of pelargonidin are more likely to appear.
Furthermore, the sweet potato plant produced had a significantly high proportion of R2 in the following structural formula (I) being a caffeic acid modifying group, which is a hydroxycinnamic acid, and was recognized to be a sweet potato plant with a genetic background suitable for the enzymatic activity responsible for the acylation of caffeic acid at that site.

Considering that there have been no reports of lines with high content of pelargonidin anthocyanins having modified groups of hydroxycinnamic acids, which are contained in the above-produced sweet potato plants, in the vast amount of past sweet potato breeding research, the phenotype related to the pigment composition expressed by the sweet potato plants of the present invention was recognized as a trait based on a characteristic genetic background that appears in the cross population obtained by crossing the above-mentioned two original varieties. Furthermore, it was presumed that it would be difficult to obtain the sweet potato plants of the present invention from sweet potato varieties other than the above-mentioned original varieties, in terms of their genetic background.





Furthermore, the sweet potato plant according to the present invention has a genetic background that enables it to accumulate a high content of pelargonidin anthocyanin compounds as "major pigments."
Here, sweet potato lines containing pelargonidin-based anthocyanins that have been reported so far include the "Borami" line in Korea (Non-Patent Document 1) and the "RSWP" line in Europe (Patent Document 1, etc.). However, these lines are sweet potato lines of a different origin from the sweet potato plant of the present invention, and when a pigment composition is prepared using these lines, it is not possible to realize color development characteristics that exhibit a vivid red color tone.
In detail, according to Non-Patent Document 1, the main component of the anthocyanin compound contained in the tuberous root of the "Borami" lineage is "peonidin-based anthocyanin", and the content of pelargonidin-based anthocyanin is only 36% (Cy:Pn:Pg=1:2.9:2.2) of all anthocyanin compounds (Non-Patent Document 1, Table 1 and page 3155). In this regard, the color development characteristics of the pigment composition of the Borami lineage were recognized as findings consistent with the fact that the color tone is a reddish purple tone different from that of red radish pigments.
In addition, the applicant carefully examined the compositional characteristics of the "RSWP" line described in Patent Document 1 (Examination Example 1 of the present specification), and found that paragraph 0084 of the Examples of Patent Document 1 described that the pelargonidin anthocyanin content of the RSWP line was only 58 mol% of all anthocyanin compounds (a value less than 70% in terms of the content ratio of all anthocyanin compounds: see Examination Example 1 of the present specification). In this respect, the RSWP line was recognized as a line with an insufficiently high pelargonidin anthocyanin content, which was also recognized as a finding consistent with the fact that the color tone was purplish red.
Furthermore, when Patent Document 1 was examined (Example 1 of the present specification), it was found that the RSWP line had a composition significantly different from that of the red sweet potato plant produced in the present invention, also in terms of the composition related to the acylation modification pattern of the anthocyanin pigment. Specifically, it was found that the RSWP line had a high content of hydroxybenzoic acid-modified anthocyanins as the main peak, and a low content of hydroxycinnamic acid-modified anthocyanins that contribute to improved stability (Patent Document 1, Examples 1 to 3 and Fig. 1, etc.).

In this specification, the term "anthocyanin compound" refers to a natural pigment compound derived from a plant material that exhibits a color tone such as red, blue, or purple, and is a glycoside in which a sugar molecule (e.g., glucose, galactose, rhamnose, etc.) is bound to the aglycone anthocyanidin. In addition to the sugar molecule, an acylated anthocyanin may be formed by binding an organic acid (e.g., sinapic acid, caffeic acid, succinic acid, malonic acid, etc.). There are many molecular species of anthocyanin compounds with different modification patterns depending on the type of aglycone, the type and binding position of the sugar molecule, and the presence or absence, type, and binding position of an organic acid. Anthocyanin compounds have various color tones and various properties that vary depending on the differences in these structures.
In this specification, "anthocyanidin" refers to the aglycone of an anthocyanin compound, and refers to a compound that binds to sugar molecules and the like to form the skeletal structure of an anthocyanin compound. The general structural formula of anthocyanidin can be shown as the following structural formula (II). R 21 to R 27 in structural formula (II) each represent any one of a hydrogen atom (-H), a hydroxyl group (-OH), or a methoxy group (-OCH 3 ).

Anthocyanidins consist of three ring structures, A ring, B ring, and C ring, and are mainly classified into six types, namely pelargonidin, cyanidin, delphinidin, peonidin, petunidin, and malvidin, depending on the number of hydroxyl groups (-OH) or methoxy groups (-OCH 3 ) added to ring B (the benzene ring on the right side of structural formula (II)). Other examples include aurantidin, luteolinidin, europinidin, and rosinidin. These have various different colors when they become the constituent aglycones of anthocyanin compounds.

[Means for Creation]
The sweet potato plant of the present invention can be produced by utilizing the genetic background obtained at a certain frequency by crossing the parent lines Kyukei 89360-8 (FERM BP-22365) and Kyukei 294 (FERM BP-22364).
That is, the sweet potato plant of the present invention is a sweet potato plant belonging to a cross population produced by crossing parent lines Kyukei 89360-8 (FERM BP-22365) and Kyukei 294 (FERM BP-22364), or belongs to a progeny population in which a sweet potato plant belonging to the cross population is used as a cross parent.




In the present invention , a sweet potato plant having a high content of pelargonidin-based anthocyanin compounds as a main pigment component, which is not contained in plants of normal sweet potato varieties, can be produced by selecting from a cross population obtained by crossing two specific original variety lines. In this specification, the term "original variety" is used in a broad sense to include parent lines that are not registered as varieties.
The sweet potato plant according to the present invention has a metabolic mechanism that inhibits the production and accumulation of cyanidin and peonidin and enables the production and accumulation of pelargonidin. On the other hand, the original varieties of the sweet potato plant according to the present invention, Kyukei 89360-8 (FERM BP-22365) and Kyukei 294 (FERM BP-22364), are varieties that contain cyanidin-based anthocyanins and peonidin-based anthocyanins as main pigments like ordinary sweet potato varieties, but are not varieties that contain pelargonidin-based anthocyanins.
However, in the process of producing the sweet potato plant with bright red tuberous roots according to the present invention, the mechanism by which the trait containing pelargonidin anthocyanins not present in the original varieties is acquired is unknown, and in the cross population resulting from the combination of these original varieties, a genetic background expressing the trait containing pelargonidin anthocyanins occurs at a certain frequency. It is presumed that the trait containing pelargonidin anthocyanins according to the present invention is a special trait expressed due to a genetic background characteristic of the cross population obtained by crossing the above two lines of the original varieties.



In addition, the sweet potato plant according to the present invention has a characteristic that the content of anthocyanin compounds in which anthocyanin compounds are acylated and modified with hydroxycinnamic acids is remarkably high. More specifically, the content of anthocyanin compounds in which R2 in the general structural formula (I) of anthocyanin compounds is acylated and modified with caffeic acid, which is a hydroxycinnamic acid, is remarkably high, and the plant has a metabolic function suitable for the enzyme activity that controls the acylation of caffeic acid at that site.
This trait is presumed to be a characteristic trait related to acylation modification properties derived from the genetic background of one of the original varieties, Kyukei 89360-8 (FERM BP-22365), and has not been found in other varieties.
Here, the original varieties, Kyukei 89360-8 (FERM BP-22365) and Kyukei 294 (FERM BP-22364), are breeding lines independently developed by the present inventors and are not publicly known or publicly available techniques.


As the above points indicate, in the present invention, by focusing on a cross population formed by combining the two lines of the above original varieties or their progeny, and performing selection using anthocyanin composition as an indicator, it is possible to produce sweet potato plants that are high in pelargonidin anthocyanin compounds.
In addition, in the vast amount of sweet potato breeding research conducted to date, there have been no reports of varieties or lines that have the trait of high content of pelargonidin-based anthocyanins having modified groups of hydroxycinnamic acids, which is possessed by the sweet potato plant of the present invention. In this regard, it is presumed that it would be difficult to obtain the sweet potato plant of the present invention from the viewpoint of its genetic background by combining sweet potato varieties or lines other than the above two original varieties.




Cross population or progeny population of original variety The sweet potato plant of the present invention belongs to a cross population or progeny population of the original variety.
Here, the "breeding population" of the original variety refers to a first generation offspring population obtained by crossbreeding the two original variety lines. The breeding population is a population created by combining the genetic backgrounds possessed by the two original variety lines, and at least some of the individuals in the breeding population include individuals that have newly acquired a genetic background of a trait that enables the accumulation of pelargonidin-based anthocyanins. Furthermore, at least some or all of the individuals in the breeding population have the trait of high content of anthocyanins having hydroxycinnamic acid modification groups.
The "progeny population" refers to a child population and its descendant population obtained using an individual belonging to the mating population as one or both of the mating parents. More specifically, examples include a self-fertilized population of individuals belonging to the mating population, a mating population between an individual belonging to the mating population and another breed line, and further mating populations of these self-fertilized populations or these with another breed line. The progeny population also includes these further progeny populations.

The sweet potato plant of the present invention can be obtained with the desired superior traits by repeating self-pollination, inbred cross-pollination, cross-breeding, selection, etc. Here, superior traits include, for example, traits with a higher pelargonidin anthocyanin content, traits with a higher anthocyanin content with hydroxycinnamic acid modification groups, traits with a higher content of anthocyanin compounds (for example, traits with a high raw material color value), traits that are advantageous for the quality of processed products and manufacturing processes described below, etc.

Compositional characteristics related to color development properties (pelargonidin anthocyanin content, etc.)
The sweet potato plant according to the present invention has a compositional characteristic of being rich in pelargonidin anthocyanin compounds with respect to the composition of anthocyanin pigments, while the sweet potato plant according to the present invention has a compositional characteristic of being low in the contents of cyanidin anthocyanins and peonidin anthocyanins, which are the main pigments in normal sweet potato plants.


The sweet potato plant according to the present invention is recognized as a sweet potato plant in which the production and accumulation of cyanidin and peonidin is suppressed and which has acquired a metabolic system that enables the production and accumulation of pelargonidin. In this respect, the sweet potato plant according to the present invention is recognized as a sweet potato plant having a genetic background capable of accumulating a pelargonidin-based anthocyanin compound excellent in bright red coloring as a main pigment.
Due to the pigment composition, the sweet potato plant of the present invention exhibits a vivid red color that cannot be expressed by ordinary sweet potato plants. The vivid red color tends to be particularly pronounced in the tuberous root. The extract of the sweet potato plant exhibits a color tone close to orange-red in the acidic range, and is very close to the color tone of radish pigment.


In general, sweet potato plants have a metabolic system in which pelargonidin anthocyanins are not synthesized and/or accumulated in vivo, and therefore, in terms of the types of anthocyanin aglycones, the anthocyanin composition is characterized by being mainly composed of cyanidin anthocyanins and peonidin anthocyanins.The coloring characteristics of the anthocyanin are purple to reddish purple in the weak acidic range, reflecting the compound composition.

The sweet potato plant according to the present invention has a compositional characteristic in terms of the composition of anthocyanin pigments, which is rich in anthocyanin compounds acylated with phenolic acids.

(4) "Various analyses of anthocyanin pigment composition"
Using the measurement data obtained from the HPLC/MS analysis described above, the compositional characteristics of the anthocyanin pigments in the root tuber extract of Kyushu No. 187 were analyzed.


Characteristics of aglycones In the above HPLC/MS analysis, an analysis was carried out focusing on the differences in the types of aglycones. From the above results, it was demonstrated that the newly developed line, Kyushu 187, is a sweet potato line with compositional characteristics regarding the basic structure of anthocyanin pigments that are completely different from those of ordinary sweet potato varieties.
In light of these results, Kyushu 187 was recognized to be a sweet potato strain in which the production and accumulation of cyanidin and peonidin was suppressed and which had acquired metabolic system functions that enabled the production and accumulation of pelargonidin.




Characteristics regarding acylation modification rate In the measurement data of the above HPLC/MS analysis, focusing on the acylation modification of anthocyanin, the sum of each peak area of compounds containing a specific modification group at the acylation modification site was calculated, and the ratio to the total peak area was calculated.
As a result, it was shown that in the anthocyanin pigments from the root extract of Kyushu 187, about 91.5% of the peak area of all anthocyanin compounds was acylated anthocyanins having phenolic acid modification groups (Table 7). In addition, as for the acylation modification mode with phenolic acid in the anthocyanin pigments, the ratio of the peak area of compounds containing hydroxybenzoic acid modification groups was a low ratio of 23.6% of the peak area of all anthocyanin compounds, while the ratio of the peak area of compounds containing hydroxycinnamic acid modification groups that act to improve stability was an extremely high value of 87.2% of the peak area of all anthocyanin compounds.
The ratio of the acylation modification type showed the same tendency when calculated for each series of compounds with each aglycone as the basic skeleton. In particular, the modification ratio of hydroxycinnamic acid showed values in the range of 86 to 90.1%, even when the aglycone skeleton structure was different, and was shown to be substantially the same as the ratio to all anthocyanin compounds (Table 7).



From the above results, it was shown that the newly developed line, Kyushu 187, is a sweet potato line that is rich in anthocyanin compounds acylated with phenolic acid, and the majority of these (about 87.2% of all anthocyanins, about 95.3% of phenolic acid-modified anthocyanins) are anthocyanin compounds containing modified groups of hydroxycinnamic acids. Hydroxycinnamic acids act to improve the stability of anthocyanin compounds, so the anthocyanin pigment from Kyushu 187 was recognized to be a pigment composition rich in anthocyanin compounds with excellent stability.
Furthermore, the acylation modification pattern showed the same tendency even among compounds with different skeletal structures and aglycones. In particular, with regard to the modification ratio of hydroxycinnamic acids, no substantial difference was observed among compounds with different skeletal structures and aglycones.
These results suggest that Kyushu 187 is a sweet potato strain with weak metabolic activity controlling acylation modification with hydroxybenzoic acid, while having significantly stronger metabolic activity controlling acylation modification with hydroxycinnamic acids.



Characteristics regarding the caffeic acid modification rate at specific modification sites In the measurement data of the above-mentioned HPLC/MS analysis, the ratio of the peak area showing the compound of each modification pattern (modification patterns A to H detected in Tables 3 to 5) defined by the combination of R2 and R3 , which are the acylation modification sites in the general structural formula (I) of anthocyanin, to the total peak area was calculated (Table 8).
As a result, it was shown that the anthocyanin pigments from the root tuber extract of Kyushu 187 had a compositional characteristic of containing a large amount of anthocyanin compounds having structures represented by modification patterns E, G, and H. Here, the structures represented by modification patterns E, G, and H had a common structure in that the acylation modification of R2 was a caffeic acid modifying group.



Therefore, the total peak area of anthocyanin compounds having a caffeic acid modifying group as R2 was calculated, and the ratio to the total peak area was calculated. The total peak area of anthocyanin compounds having no caffeic acid modifying group as R2 was also calculated in the same manner, and the ratio to the total peak area was calculated (Table 9).
As a result, the total peak area of the anthocyanin compounds having a caffeic acid modifying group as R2 accounted for 80.4% of the peak area of all anthocyanin compounds. The ratio of the modification type showed the same tendency when calculated for each series of compounds having each aglycone as a basic skeleton, and was found to be close to the ratio for all anthocyanin compounds.
On the other hand, the total peak area of the anthocyanin compounds not having a caffeic acid modifying group as R2 was only 19.6% of the peak area of all the anthocyanin compounds.


From the above results, it was shown that the newly developed line, Kyushu 187, is a sweet potato line that is highly enriched (80% or more) in anthocyanin compounds in which R2 , the acylation modification site of the general structural formula (I) of anthocyanin, is acylated with caffeic acid. Since caffeic acid is a type of hydroxycinnamic acid that acts to improve the stability of anthocyanin compounds, the anthocyanin pigment from Kyushu 187 was recognized to be a pigment composition that is highly enriched in anthocyanin compounds with excellent compound stability. In addition, the modification pattern of caffeic acid at the R2 site showed the same tendency even in compounds with different aglycone skeleton structures.
These results suggest that Kyushu 187 is a sweet potato line with strong metabolic activity in which R2 in the general structural formula (I) of anthocyanin is acylated with caffeic acid.
