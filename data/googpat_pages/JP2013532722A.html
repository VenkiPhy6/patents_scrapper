Tuberculosis that affects the lungs is known as pulmonary tuberculosis. Pulmonary tuberculosis is the most prevalent form of tuberculosis (Tuberculosis, 2005, 85, p. 227-234). Current chemotherapeutic regimens for treating pulmonary tuberculosis include co-administration of primary anti-tuberculosis drugs (isoniazid, rifampicin, esambutol and / or pyrazinamide) for 4 months, followed by isoniazid, rifampicin and / or esambutol 2 Depending on the type of tuberculosis, the treatment period can be further extended and can range from 9 months to 2 years.
