Process for producing hydroxylated cholesterol by biological transformation and dihydroxycholesterol

Abstract
translated from

A method for preparing at least one hydroxycholesterol chosen from the group consisting of 25-hydroxycholesterol, 17, 25-dihydroxycholesterol and 25, 26-dihydroxycholesterol by biological hydroxylation of cholesterol, and the aforementioned dihydroxycholesterols. In the above biological hydroxylation, a microorganism is used which has the abovementioned hydroxylation capacity and which belongs to the genus Amycolata and the genus Sphingomonas.

Technical field
The present invention relates to a method for hydroxylating cholesterol by the action of microorganisms, more specifically, any one or more of 25-hydroxycholesterol, 17,25-dihydroxycholesterol or 25,26-dihydroxycholesterol from cholesterol. It relates to a manufacturing method. It also relates to the novel dihydroxycholesterol.
Background art
Biological methods, particularly methods for producing hydroxy derivatives of steroids such as cholesterol using microorganisms, include Streptomyces (StreptomycesJP-A-7-123997 discloses a method for converting cholesterol to 25-hydroxycholesterol using a genus microorganism. Interesting biological conversions of compounds other than steroids include vitamin D-type microorganisms such as Nocardia AutotroNocardia autotropica), Streptomyces roseosporus (Streptomyces roseosporus), Amicolata Saturnea (Amycolata saturnea), Amicolata autotrohika (Amycolata autotropica), Sphingomonas sp (Sphingomonas  sp. ) Is known for producing 25-hydroxyvitamin D by hydroxylation (JP-A-4-166090, JP-A-7-241197).
On the other hand, it is known that cholesterol can be an intermediate in chemical synthesis of various vitamin Ds (organic synthetic chemistry).37809-829 (1979), a compound obtained by hydroxylating one or more specific sites of cholesterol in advance can be expected to be used as a synthetic intermediate for various hydroxylated vitamin Ds. Certainly, according to the conversion method using microorganisms described in the above-mentioned JP-A-7-123997, it has been disclosed that the preferred cholesterol at position 25 can be selectively hydroxylated in relation to active vitamin D.
However, according to the method described in the publication, the hydroxylation efficiency is not always satisfactory. From the viewpoint of improving the water solubility of the final compound derived from cholesterol, for example, vitamin Ds, not only mono-hydroxylated cholesterol but also further hydroxylated, for example, dihydroxylated cholesterol can be provided. Would be desired.
Accordingly, an object of the present invention is to provide an efficient method for producing mono-hydroxylated cholesterol, particularly 25-hydroxycholesterol, as well as a method for producing further hydroxylated dihydroxycholesterol and novel dihydroxycholesterol itself. .

Detailed description of the invention
In the biological transformation of the present invention, amicolata (Amycolata) Genus and Sphingomonas (Sphingomonas) Microorganisms belonging to the genus, which have the ability to convert the cholesterol of formula (I) into the hydroxylated cholesterol of formula (II), and their cultured cell preparations, species and strain types Can be used without asking. Preferred microorganisms include the above-mentioned Amicholata Saturnea (Amycolata saturnea), Especially microorganisms deposited at the Biotechnology Institute of Industrial Technology, Ministry of International Trade and Industry, Ministry of International Trade and Industry under the accession numbers FERM BP-5544 and FERM BP-2307,Amycolata autotropica), In particular, strains deposited with the American Type Culture Collection under the accession number ATCC 33796.
In addition, although the conversion ability is inferior to the conversion ability of the microorganism belonging to the genus Amycolata, Sphingomonas (Sphingomonas) Species belonging to the genus, Marie (mari) IFO 15500, pouch mobilis (paucimobilis) IFO 13935, Parapauchimobilis (parapaucimobilis) IFO 15100, Yanoikuyae (Yanoikuyae) IFO 15102, Adhaesiba (adhaesiva) IFO 15099, Capsula (capsula) IFO 12533, Sangis (sanguis) IFO 13937, Macro Goltabidas (macrogortabidus) IFO 15033 and Terrae (terrae) IFO 15098 can also be used. The IFO is a deposit number at the Institute for Fermentation in Japan.
According to the present invention, in the presence of any of these strains or cultured cells thereof and oxygen, the starting material (or substrate) cholesterol (compound of formula (I)) and / or 25-hydroxycholesterol (formula (Compound (II-a)) is incubated. This treatment is performed by culturing the strain under an aerobic condition by adding a substrate to the culture solution, or in some cases, the cultured cells of the strain may be prepared, for example, as they are or as a homogenized preparation. It can also be carried out by adding the substrate to the suspension and incubating with aeration of oxygen, eg air. The substrate may be added to the culture solution either before culturing or after a certain period of time has elapsed after culturing. The cells can be produced by inoculating any one of the above strains on a nutrient source-containing medium and culturing aerobically.
Cultivation of a strain for preparing a preparation of such cultured cells or culturing of a strain performed in a state where a substrate is added can be performed in principle according to a general microorganism culture method. It is preferable to practice under aerobic conditions such as shaking culture by liquid culture and aeration and agitation culture.
As a medium used for the culture, Amicolata (Amycolata) Genus and Sphingomonas (Sphingomonas) Any medium containing a nutrient source that can be used by microorganisms belonging to the genus can be used, and any of various synthetic, semi-synthetic or natural media can be used. As the medium composition, glucose, maltose, xylose, fructose, sucrose, etc. as a carbon source can be used alone or in combination. As the nitrogen source, peptone, meat extract, soybean powder, casein, amino acid, yeast extract, organic nitrogen sources such as urea, and inorganic nitrogen sources such as sodium nitrate and ammonium sulfate can be used alone or in combination. In addition, for example, salts such as sodium chloride, potassium chloride, calcium carbonate, magnesium sulfate, sodium phosphate, potassium phosphate, and cobalt chloride, heavy metal salts, and vitamins can be added and used as necessary. In addition, when foaming is remarkable during culture, various known antifoaming agents can be appropriately added to the medium.
The culture conditions can be appropriately selected within a range where the strain can grow well. Usually, it is cultured for about 2 to 8 days at pH 6 to 7.5 and 28 to 30 C. The various culture conditions described above can be appropriately changed according to the type and characteristics of microorganisms used, external conditions, etc., and those skilled in the art will be able to easily select optimum conditions.
In addition, a cultured cell preparation is prepared by suspending a cell separated by centrifugation or filtration or a homogenized cell after culturing in an appropriate solution. The solution that can be used for suspending the cells is the above-mentioned medium, or a buffer such as Tris-acetic acid, Tris-hydrochloric acid, sodium succinate, sodium citrate, sodium phosphate, potassium phosphate or the like alone or in combination. It is a thing. The pH of the buffer solution is preferably 6.0 to 9.0, more preferably 7.0 to 8.5.
The substrate can be left as a powder or dissolved in a water-soluble organic solvent such as ethanol and added to a culture solution or a suspension of bacterial cells. 0.15 to 0.60 mg per ml is preferred. If the amount added is more than 0.60 mg / ml, the conversion rate is slow, which is not preferable. After the addition of the substrate, the target is hydroxylated by subjecting the substrate to a target aerobic condition by performing an operation such as shaking or aeration stirring at 27 to 31 C. for 1 to 3 days, preferably about 1 day. Can be converted to cholesterol. In the conversion reaction, the conversion rate from the substrate to the target hydroxylated cholesterol can be dramatically increased by allowing the substrate and the methylated cyclodextrin to coexist in the reaction solution.
Therefore, in a preferred embodiment of the present invention, the incubation treatment is further performed in the presence of methylated cyclodextrins.
The methylated cyclodextrins used in the present invention are compounds in which the hydrogen atom of the hydroxyl group at the 2, 3 or 6 position of the cyclodextrin is substituted with a methyl group, and is completely methylated at the 2 and 6 positions. -Hexakis- (2,6-O-dimethyl) --cyclodextrin derived from cyclodextrin, heptakis- (2,6-O-dimethyl) --cyclodextrin derived from -cyclodextrin and -cyclodextrin Octakis- (2,6-O-dimethyl) --cyclodextrin or hexakis- (2,3,6-O-trimethyl derived from -cyclodextrin fully methylated at positions 2, 3 and 6 ) --cyclodextrin, -cyclodextrin-derived heptakis- (2,3,6-O-trimethyl) --cyclo Oxakis- (2,3,6-O-trimethyl) --cyclodextrin derived from xistrin and -cyclodextrin, or 6, 7 or 8 hydroxyl groups in 2, 3, 6 positions partially Mention may be made of methylated partially methylated cyclodextrins. In the present invention, any one or two or more of the above methylated cyclodextrins are selected and used. In particular, a partially methylated cyclodextrin derived from -cyclodextrin is preferably used.
The amount of methylated cyclodextrins added is 0.5 mg or more, preferably 0.5 to 15 mg, more preferably 1 to 10 mg per 1 ml of the reaction solution. When the amount of methylated cyclodextrins added is less than 0.5 mg per 1 ml of the reaction solution, the increase in the conversion rate to the target hydroxylated cholesterol may not be significant compared to the additive-free one, If the addition amount is around 15 mg, foaming may occur in some cases, and it may be necessary to use an antifoaming agent or the like.
Moreover, in the method of this invention, you may add a nonionic surfactant to a reaction liquid in the range which does not reduce the said conversion rate. As such a surfactant, polyoxyethylene sorbitan fatty acid ester (for example, Tween 80 (manufactured by Sigma)), sorbitan fatty acid ester (for example, Span 85 (manufactured by Sigma)), polyoxyethylene ether (for example, Brij96 (manufactured by Sigma) )), Triton X-100 (manufactured by Sigma), nonylphenol (for example, Nonipol 45 (manufactured by Sanyo Chemical Industries),) block copolymer of ethylene oxide-propylene oxide (for example Pluronic L-61 (Asahi Denka Kogyo Co., Ltd.) )), And anionic surfactants include Dairex (manufactured by NOF Corporation), Trax (manufactured by NOF Corporation), and the like.
In order to isolate the desired hydroxylated cholesterol thus produced from the reaction mixture, various known purification means can be selected and combined. For example, adsorption to hydrophobic adsorption resin, elution, solvent extraction using ethyl acetate, n-butanol, etc., column method using silica gel or thin layer chromatography, preparative high performance liquid chromatography using reverse phase column, etc. Can be separated and purified by using them alone or in appropriate combination, and optionally repeatedly.
