Enzyme-containing oral composition for animals and process for producing the same

Abstract
translated from

An oral composition for animals which contains thermostable enzymes having a residual activity of 60 % or above after being heated at 80 C for 20 seconds. It is useful as feed, food or drug that can be taken through the mouths of animals including humans, fish and birds and that are improved in functionality, nutritive value, storability, and in vivo digestibility.

 FIELD OF THE INVENTION
 The present invention relates to a composition for animal oral administration containing an enzyme and a method for producing the same. More specifically, the use of heat-resistant enzymes suppresses the degradation of enzymes in the manufacturing process and improves storage stability. It relates to the manufacturing method. Background art
 Enzymes have been used as one of feed additives. For example, various enzymes such as protease, amylase, xylanase, cellulase, glucanase, and galactosidase have been added to and mixed with various feeds. One of its uses is to promote the digestion of livestock when feed is consumed. Another use is to promote the decomposition of fiber during silage treatment of feed crops to produce good lactic acid fermentation.
 These enzymes may be added to the feed when feeding livestock, but the amount of the enzyme is very small in the feed, and it is generally difficult to mix a small amount of the enzyme uniformly in the feed. . Also, when the enzyme in the fine powder is mixed into the feed, the enzyme fine particles may be scattered and may adversely affect the feeder. Spraying the feed as an aqueous solution is conceivable, but the degradation of the enzyme tends to proceed in the aqueous solution. Therefore, it is desirable to add enzymes to the livestock in the form of a mixed feed containing the enzymes uniformly at the feed production stage.
6 5 C 0 C However, enzymes are generally susceptible to inactivation and decomposition at high temperatures. Production of compound feed The production process includes high-temperature treatment steps for the purpose of sterilization, drying, etc., and inevitably decomposes and deactivates enzymes. In particular, in recent feed production methods, heated granulators such as pellet mills or extruders have been used to improve moldability and handleability. The degree of enzyme inactivation is relatively small when processing under relatively mild temperature conditions of less than 65 C, but there is no problem, but these heating granulators that process at a high temperature of 0 C or more Most of the enzymes are broken down in the feed production process. For this reason, there is a need for the development of a technology that allows enzymes to be stably present in compound feeds produced through a high-temperature treatment process.
 As a compound feed manufacturing process that avoids the inactivation of the enzyme, the enzyme containing the enzyme is coated by applying a slurry or solution containing the enzyme to the surface of a feed pellet that has been subjected to high-temperature treatment steps such as sterilization, drying, and molding. There is a way to do it. In this method, an appropriate enzyme slurry is prepared, a special device for coating the slurry is required, and a drying step after coating is required.In this case, the enzyme is decomposed or deactivated. For example, Japanese Patent Application Laid-Open No. 6-504911 proposes a method of coating an enzyme with a coating material containing a high melting point fat or wax and calcium carbonate or kaolin. For example, Japanese Patent Application Laid-Open No. 63-157938 discloses an enzyme premix obtained by mixing an enzyme with a carrier such as flour. However, in all of these cases, the strength of the enzyme preparations is not always sufficient, and there is a possibility that the enzymatic preparations may be crushed in the crushing step of the feed production process, etc. In addition, direct contact with heat or moisture will deactivate the enzyme and its effect is likely to be drastically reduced. In addition, the enzyme premix is a premix that matches the enzyme. This process is necessary, and its stability is not at a sufficient level in a manufacturing process using heating equipment.
7 0 C  Furthermore, some feeds, such as feed for fishery products and feed for silkworms, have a high water content.They have a shape-retaining property, and the water-soluble nutrients in the feed are quickly eluted. Having. However, enzymes are generally highly stable in the dry state and have excellent storage stability, but under conditions of high water content, the stability is low and deactivation is likely to occur. In particular, when processing is performed at 70 C or more to form pellets, the inactivation of the enzyme is further accelerated. For this reason, it is very difficult to incorporate enzymes into feeds with a high water content to exert their original effects.
 In addition, in the case of enzyme-containing feed, the enzyme is deactivated during storage at room temperature. For this reason, it is usually necessary to use the product in a short period of time after production from the viewpoint of maintaining quality, which leads to complications in quality control and an increase in costs due to transportation in small quantities. Therefore, in order to simplify quality control and reduce transportation costs, there is a need for an enzyme-containing feed that can maintain enzyme activity for a long time immediately after production.
 Therefore, an object of the present invention is to provide a composition containing an enzyme as a component of an oral composition for animals such as feed, foodstuffs, medicines, etc., in which the enzyme is not deactivated even after a production process using a high-temperature heating device. Another object of the present invention is to provide a composition for oral use in animals which is stable even for long-term storage and a method for producing the same. Disclosure of the invention
The present inventors have found that the present invention has an effect that it is not decomposed even in the process of producing a molded composition using a high-temperature heating type device, and is effective against a wide range of domestic animals such as livestock, poultry, and pet farmed animals.
