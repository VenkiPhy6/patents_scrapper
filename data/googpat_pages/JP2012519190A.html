Antibodies and epitopes specific to misfolded prion proteins

Abstract
translated from Japanese

The present invention relates to antibodies and immunogenic peptides specific to misfolded prion proteins (PrP, eg, PrP Sc ) and uses thereof. The immunogenic peptide comprises the amino acid sequence tyrosine-methionine-leucine (YML). This antibody or peptide can be used to treat or prevent diseases or disorders associated with misfolded PrP, including cancer. Specifically, an IgM monoclonal antibody designated 1A1 was generated using a peptide consisting of the sequence GGYMLGS (ie, SEQ ID NO: 8), corresponding to residues 126-132 of human PrP. 1A1 recognizes PrP folded in error, but does not recognize normal PrP.

Prion refers to an agent composed largely and possibly alone of a single protein, Prion protein or PrP. Misfolded prion protein (misfolded PrP) has been associated with various diseases. Normal cellular prion protein is commonly referred to as PrP C, misfolded protease-resistant isoform called PrP Sc. PrP has been identified in several species, including mammalian and avian species.   The term epitope refers to the sequence of amino acids in a protein, or modifications thereto (eg, glycosylation). Amino acids can be arranged linearly, such as the primary sequence of a protein, or can be a secondary or tertiary sequence of adjacent amino acids once the protein is partially or fully composed. An epitope can be specifically bound by an antibody, antibody fragment, peptide, peptidomimetic or the like, or can be specifically bound by a ligand. Epitopes can have a variety of sizes, for example, linear epitopes can be as small as 2 amino acids or larger from about 3 amino acids to about 20 amino acids. In some embodiments, an epitope can be from about 5 amino acids to about 10 or about 15 amino acids in length. The epitope of the secondary or tertiary amino acid sequence can include as few as 2 amino acids, or can be larger, from about 3 amino acids to about 20 amino acids. In some embodiments, the secondary or tertiary epitope can be from about 5 amino acids to about 10 or about 15 amino acids in close proximity to some or other of the epitopes.

An isoform is any of several different forms of the same protein.
