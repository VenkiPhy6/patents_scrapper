Microorganism with cadaveric amine productivity and method for producing cadaverine using the same

The present invention relates to a microorganism for producing a diamine and a method of producing a diamine using the microorganism.

(BA)(decarboxylation)(spermidine)(spermine)(putrescine)1,4-(cadaverine) The living amine (BA) is a nitrogen-containing compound which is mainly produced by decarboxylation of an amino acid or by amination of an aldehyde and a ketone and transamination. These biogenic amines are low molecular weight compounds and are synthesized in the metabolism of microorganisms, plants and animals, and thus biogenic amines are known to be components frequently found in such cells. In particular, the biogenic amine is a polyamine such as spermidine, spermine, putrescine or 1,4-butanediamine and cadaverine.
