TECHNICAL FIELD The present invention relates to a novel fermentation accelerator for lactic acid bacteria.

In the industrial production of fermented products and metabolites by microorganisms, shortening the fermentation time is important for reducing production costs and sanitary control. As a technique for promoting the fermentation of lactic acid bacteria to shorten the fermentation time, it has been reported that a substance serving as a raw material for nucleic acid promotes the fermentation of lactic acid bacteria (Patent Document 1). Further, Patent Document 2 describes a microbial productivity improver containing an organic acid extract of cruciferous plant seeds or the like as an active ingredient. However, from the viewpoint of efficiently producing fermented milk on an industrial scale, there is still a need for new technical means for simply promoting the metabolism and fermentation action of lactic acid bacteria.

On the other hand, with the recent increase in health consciousness, it is required to reduce the amount of food additives used to produce fermented milk with good taste. In particular, succinic acid is often used as a food additive because it is an umami substance and also has useful physiological functions such as weight gain suppressing action, glucose tolerance improving action, and cancer growth suppressing action. From the viewpoint of stimulating consumer demand, it is preferable to produce succinic acid in fermented milk without using food additives.

Recently, regarding the production mechanism of succinate, it has been reported that under anaerobic conditions, oxaloacetate is generated from pyruvate or phosphoenolpyruvate through carbonic acid fixation, and succinate is sometimes produced in the reverse route of the TCA cycle. (Non-Patent Document 1). However, there is no report that lactic acid bacteria can utilize the reverse route of the TCA cycle.

The present invention provides new technical means for promoting fermentation of lactic acid bacteria. Moreover, the present invention provides a new technical means for promoting the production of succinic acid by lactic acid bacteria in fermented milk.

Furthermore, the present inventors have found that the organic acid can promote the production of succinic acid by lactic acid bacteria in fermented milk. The present invention is based on such findings.
