Ethanol resistance saccharomyces cerevisiae gp-01 by protoplast fusion, method for manufacturing thereof, method for manufacturing yeast containing high content of bio organic germanium by using saccharomyces cerevisiae gp-01 and high water soluble sodium metagermanate as a germanium source

Abstract
translated from

The present invention relates to a method for producing yeast containing the high content of organic bio-germanium by using yeast with ethanol-resistant and Sodium metagermanate(Na2 GeO3) by protoplast fusion. In order to enhance the resistance against ethanol which inhibits the growth of yeast, the present invention provides a method for producing a mutated Sac- charomyces Cervisiae(ye,d&t GP-Ol, KCTC 11399BP) which is ethanol-resistant and obtained by protoplast fusing Saccharomyces Cerevisiae(KCTC 7904) and Candida Ethanolica(KCTC 7181). Further, the present invention provides yeast containing the high content of organic bio- germanium(the yeast Ge-32K) and a method for producing the yeast Ge-32K, comprising steps of adding the yeast GP-Ol into a solution of sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) at the volume ratio of 1:0.5-2; adding 0.1~0.4wt% of surfactant, instead of germanium dioxide as used in the prior arts; and cultivating an obtained broth. The obtained yeast Ge-32K contains the higher content of the organic bio-germanium than the conventional yeast as produced by using germanium dioxide.

Description
ETHANOL RESISTANCE SACCHAROMYCES CEREVISIAE GP-Ol BY PROTOPLAST FUSION, METHOD FOR MANUFACTURING THEREOF, METHOD FOR MANUFACTURING YEAST CONTAINING HIGH CONTENT OF BIO ORGANIC GERMANIUM BY USING SACCHAROMYCES CEREVISIAE
GP-Ol AND HIGH WATER SOLUBLE SODIUM METAGERMANATE AS A GERMANIUM SOURCE Technical Field
[1] The present invention relates to yeast which is ethanol resistant and contains the high content of organic bio-germanium, and the method for producing the yeast. More specifically, the method comprises producing a mutated Saccharomyces cervisiae, i.e., yeast GP-Ol by protoplast fusion of Saccharomyces cervme(KCTC7904) and Candida ethanolica(KCTC7181), the yeast being resistant against the high concentrate ethanol; cultivating the yeast GP-Ol with sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) for germanium to be inoculated into the yeast GP-Ol by the biotransformation; and finally obtaining a mutated yeast being ethanol-resistant and containing the high content of organic bio-germanium (hereinafter referred to as the yeast Ge-32K).
Background Art
[2] Germanium is classified with organic and inorganic germanium(GeO2). It is known the inorganic germanium accumulated in the human organs over the long time leads to the strong toxicity.
[3] Organic germanium is naturally contained in microorganisms, mineral water and medicinal herbs. Further, it can be made by bio-synthesized in the fungal body or chemically synthesized. The organic germanium is organically bound to the proteins in the cell, peptides, etc. and the therapeutic benefits of the organic germanium for enhancing immunity; suppressing the origination and spread of the tumor cells (metastasis), treating and preventing various chronic diseases, etc. are established by numerous papers and scientific journals (Kor. J. Microbiol. Biotechnol., 2007. 35(2):118-127; J. Kor. Soc. Food Sci. Nutr. 2006. 35(6):683-689; J. Kor. Soc. Appl. Biol. Chem. 2005.48(3):246-251; Immune Network. 2006. 6(2):86-92). In addition, the organic germanium is well-known for anticancer activities by oxygen supply to cells (J. Orthomol. Medicin., 1986. 1:145-148), purification of blood (Medicin. Hy- phothesis., 1988. 26:207-215), reinforcement of immunity by activation of NK cell and macrophage (Microbiol. Immunol., 1985. 29:65-74; Kor. J. Microbiol. Biotechnol., 2007. 35(2):118-127); inducement of interferon production (Gantokagakutyoho, 9:1976-1980); suppression of generating and spreading the cancer cell (Cancer and Chemotheraphy. 1985. 12(12):2345-2351); suppression of arthritis (Autonomic & Autacoid Pharmacol. 2005. 25:129-134); prevention and treatment of various diseases such as neuralgia (Biotechnol. Appl. Biochem., 1986. 8:379-386), osteoporosis (Germanium; The health and life enhancer. 1988); mitigation of high blood pressure (hypertension) and hyperlipidemia cholesterol (Pharmacol., 1990. 41:286-291 ); alleviation of liver toxin (J. Kor. Soc. Food. Nutr., 1994. 23(4): 581-586); and so forth. Due to the well-known therapeutic benefits, many researches on organic germanium have been widely undertaken for decades in an attempt to apply it for treatment of various obstinate diseases such as cancer and cardiopathy, etc. in U.S.A., Japan, Europe and Korea (Anticancer Res., 1985. 5(5):479-483).
[4] The organic germanium can be obtained by extracting from medicinal herbs like ginseng, ganoderma, etc., but such a method is difficult to be commercialized due to high cost and low productivity. Further, the organic germanium can be obtained by chemically synthesizing germanium dioxide (GeO2) with organic acids, but the product obtained from the chemical synthesis comprises the inorganic germanium and thus the use for the foods or medical supplies is limited due to the un-established safety (Import Alert by US FDA, 1988).
[5] In fact, the yeast is importantly used in baking, confectionery and brewery industry for thousands of years. Further, the yeast itself is useful as a source of nutrition for human being and attractive as a next generation protein source, a single cell protein (SCP) which is ideally comprised of protein, vitamin, mineral, etc. and characterized in the high protein and low fat.
[6] Furthermore, the yeast is the best source of Vitamin B among the single cells and comprising of lots of enzymes etc., which are mainly served in vivo metabolism. In this respect, the yeast is used as the health supplementary product. Recently, a large number of researches and developments on the health supplement products have been made so that the physiological and pharmaceutical benefits by the yeast are obtained. In order to achieve the said object, the development for the yeast containing the high contents of organic bio-germanium is highly required.
[7] In the conventional methods, the yeast containing the organic bio-germanium is produced by cultivating the yeast in the medium including inorganic germanium(GeO2 ). Due to the drawback that the growth of yeast is suppressed in the condition of the medium where of the high content of the inorganic germanium is contained, it is difficult in the conventional methods to produce the yeast containing the appropriate amount of organic bio-germanium in aspect of the cost-effective and scale. [8] To remedy the above drawback, a method separately executing the process of producing the yeast in the form of a pellet and the process of inoculating germanium dioxide(GeO2) in the obtained yeast is tried. However, in the above method, the yield of the palletized yeast is too low to be commercialized due to the ethanol generated during the cultivation of yeast and the content of germanium dioxide(GeO2) inoculated into the yeast is extremely low.
[9] Also it has been known that the solubility of germanium dioxide(GeO2) used in the above method is 5.2g/L at 250C, which is served as the suppresser of the growth of yeast(J. Ind. Microbiol. Biotechnol., 1989. 4(4):299-306).
[10] Therefore, it is highly required to invent the method to produce the ethanol resistant yeast containing the high content of organic bio-germanium by using the inorganic germanium which has higher solubility but much lower toxicity than germanium dioxide(GeO2). Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium, obtained by biosynthesis of the mutated saccharomyces cervisiae (ethanol resistant yeast GP-Ol) and sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03).
[13] It is a further object of the present invention to provide the yeast Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium, obtained by bio-synthesis of the mutated saccharomyces cervisiae(ye&st GP-Ol, KCTC-11399BP) and sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03). Technical Solution
[14] The present invention is related to a mutated saccharomyces cervisiae (yeast GP-Ol,
KCTCl 1399BP) being ethanol-resistant and having an ability to produce the organic germanium and a method for producing the yeast Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium, comprising of cultivating the said yeast GP-Ol with sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03).
Advantageous Effects
[15] According to the present invention, a large number of yeast GP-Ol can be produced by protoplast fusion of saccharomyces cerevisiae(KCTC-7904) and Candida ethanolica(KCTC 7181). The obtained yeast GP-Ol is ethanol-resistant, which enables to be cultivated under the high ethanol condition. The ethanol-resistant yeast GP-Ol of the present invention can overcome the drawback of the prior arts that the growth yield of the yeast is decreased due to the ethanol generated during the cultivation of yeast.
[16] Furthermore, as the productivity of the yeast GP-Ol in cultivation is increased more than 3 times compared to that of the initial yeast(KCTC-7904), the mass-production of the yeast Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium can be obtained by using the yeast GP-Ol of the present invention.
[17] In the process of inoculating germanium into pelletized yeast GP-Ol, the present invention uses sodium metagerminate(Na2Ge03) with solubility of 500 g/L, instead of germanium dioxide(GeO2) commonly used in the prior arts, and thus the rate of biotransformation of germanium in the yeast is increased owing to the higher solubility of germanium and ion transmissibility, and lower toxicity of germanium than the germanium dioxide. It is, therefore, possible for the present invention to obtain the yeast Ge-32K containing the organic bio-germanium that its contents is 2 or 3 times higher than that of the prior arts where the germanium dioxide(GeO2) is used.
[18] Also, in the process of cultivating the yeast GP-01 (KCTC 11399BP) with sodium metagerminate(Na2Ge03), the present invention adds surfactant for the organic bio- germanium to be more effectively inoculated into the pelletized yeast by the biotransformation. Brief Description of Drawings

[22] The present invention relates to a mutated Saccharomyces cervisiae, i.e., yeast GP-

[23] The mutated Saccharomyces cervisiae, yeast GP-Ol was duly deposited in Korea
Collection for Type Culture of Korea Research Institute of Bioscience and Biotechnology under the deposit number of KCTC 11399BP.
[24] More specifically, the present invention provides the mutated Saccharomyces cervisiaeiyeast GP-Ol, KCTC 11399BP) with ethanol-resistant, wherein the method comprises of protoplast fusing Saccharomyces cervisiae (KCTC7904) with Candida ethanolica(KCTC7 l&l) having the effective utilization of ethanol.
[25] Further, the present invention provides the yeast Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium, which can be produced by cultivating the yeast GP-Ol with sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) solution so as for the germanium to be inoculated into the yeast by bio-transformation.
[26] The mutated yeast can be obtained by protoplast fusion of Saccharomyces cervisiae
(KCTC7904) and Candida ethanolica(KCTC7 ISl) with a solution mixing IM sorbitol as osmotic pressure stabilizer and 0.5M of 2-mercaptoethanol as reducing agent.
[27] And, a protoplast can be obtained by suspending of the pelletized yeast in sorbitol solution that a cell wall hydrolase was added, for the cell wall to be resolved. In this case, the preferable volume ratio of IM sorbitol and 2-mercaptoethanol is 1: 2-4, more preferably, 1: 2-3.
[28] Also, the protoplast fusion is accomplished by adding two protoplasts of Saccharomyces cervisiae (KCTC7904) and Candida ethanolica (KCTC7181) into the mixed solution comprised of polyethylene glycol, MW 4000; 1OmM of calcium chloride (CaCl2); and one selected solution among IM sorbitol, sucrose and mannitol.
[29] In this case, the preferable volume ratio of the one selected solution (I M sorbitol), polyethylene glycol and calcium chloride (CaCl2) is 1:2-4:2-5, more preferably, 1:2-3: 2-4.
[30] The present invention provides a method for producing the yeast Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium. The method is comprised of cultivating the yeast GP-Ol with sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03).
[31 ] The preferable volume ratio of the yeast GP-01 (KCTC 11399BP) and sodium metagermanate (Na2GeO3) is 1:0.5-2, more preferably, 1:1.
[32] In addition, it is preferable to cultivate the yeast GP-Ol(KCTC 11399BP) with sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) for 19-21 hours at pH 9-10 and temperature 30~40C in order for the germanium to be inoculated into the yeast by the biotransformation. The above cultivating step may be accomplished by the ordinary method of cultivating yeast.
[33] By the said method of the present invention, the yeast Ge-32K containing relatively high content of organic bio-germanium can be obtained.
[34] Hereinafter, the more detailed methods of the present invention for producing the yeast GP-Ol with ethanol-resistant and for producing the yeast Ge-32K containing the high content of organic bio-germanium are provided.
[35]
[36] 1st Process: Producing a protoplast fused yeast with ethanol-resistant
[37] A protoplast fused yeastwas obtained by fusing and cultivating two protoplasts of
Saccharomyces cervme (KCTC7904) and Candida ethanolica (KCTC7181), which were deposited at Korea Collection for Type Culture (KCTC) of Korea Research Institute of Bioscience and Biotechnology.
[38] In more detail, Saccharomyces cervisiae(KCTC7904) and Candida ethanolica (KCTC7181) were cultivated, respectively. The obtained culture mediums were cen- trifuged to obtain the pelletized yeasts.
[39] It is preferable for the centrifugations of the culture mediums to be carried out when the optical density(O.D.) of the culture mediums is 0.45-0.50 at early logarithmic phase. The palletized yeasts can be obtained from either each of the mediums of the strain or one medium where the two strains are cultured together.
[40] At the ratio of 1:1, the two pelletized yeasts were added in a solution, which IM of sorbitol and 0.5M of 2-mercaptoethanol are mixed, in order to wash the yeasts twice. The washed yeasts were left during the pre-determined period and then, a solution containing cell wall hydrolase was added to a broth (containing palletized yeasts, IM of sorbitol and 0.5M of 2-mercaptoethanol) at the ratio of 1:1 The yeasts were suspended in the broth to produce protoplasts.
[41] IM sorbitol, polyethylene glycol(MW 4000) and 1OmM of calcium chloride (CaCl2) were added the broth for the protoplast to be fused. Finally, the broth was centrifuged to obtain the protoplast fused yeasts.
[42] The protoplast fused yeast was cultivated at an agar culture medium for 6-8 days and staining them to check whether to identify the protoplast fused yeast. After, the protoplast fused yeast which is confirmed by the staining was collected.
[43] Preferably, the used agar culture medium is composed of 3~5wt% of yeast extract,
7-9% of peptone, 7~9wt% of glucose, 0.1~0.6wt% of calcium chloride, 72~75wt% of sorbitol and 5~8wt% of agar, but is not limited to the above. The agar ordinary used in the art can be also applied.
[44]
[45] 2nd Process: Producing the ethanol resistant yeast which has a high adaptability against a high concentration of germanium(yeast GP-Ol, (KCTC 11399BP)), and manufacturing a solution of sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03).
[46] The protoplast fused yeast as obtained from the 1st process was cultivated in the medium containing sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) to enhance the adaptability against the high concentration of germanium of the yeast.
[47] In order to produce the yeast GP-Ol(KCTC 11399BP), sodium metagermanate (Na2
GeO3) was used for the germanium-adaptability. The protoplast fused yeast as obtained from the 1st process was cultivated in liquid culture medium for 10-13 hour in order for the concentration of germanium to be 3,000-20,000 ppm.
[48] Preferably, the liquid culture medium is composed of 6~10wt% of ethanol,
0.1~0.5wt% of peptone, 0.1~0.4wt% of yeast extract, 2~5wt% of glucose, 0.1~0.4wt% of malt extract and 84~90wt% of water, but is not limited to the above.
[49] Preferably, the optimum concentration of ethanol is 8~10wt% and the content of germanium in sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) is 3,000- 10,000ppm in the liquid culture medium.
[50] In case where the concentration of ethanol is less than 6wt% or over 10wt%, it may lead to the difficulties with obtaining the optimum quantity of the yeast or collecting the ethanol resistant yeast efficiently. Further, in case where the content of germanium is less than 3,000ppm in sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03), it is difficult to collect the yeast containing the high content of germanium effectively and in case where the content of germanium is over 20,000ppm in sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03), it is also difficult to obtain the cost-effective yield.
[51] In the liquid culture medium, the yeasts which are actively-grown and have the lower content of germanium in the supernatant of the culture were collected. The collected yeasts were sub-cultured in a plate medium to isolate and obtain a mutated Sac- charomyces cervisiae, i.e., yeast GP-Ol(KCTC 11399BP) .
[52] As the obtained yeast GP-Ol has the high resistance against ethanol, it is possible for the yield of the yeast to be increased.
[53] Preferably, the plate medium as used above was composed of 3~5wt% of agar,
10~12wt% of yeast extract, 20~23wt% of peptone, 20~23wt% of glucose and 39~42wt% of ethanol, wherein the ethanol was added before solidifying the plate, but is not limited to the above.
[54] The prepared plate was double sealed at the room temperature to prevent ethanol from being evaporated, after the subculture of the yeasts.
[55] Sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) used as the inorganic germanium in the present invention was synthesized by adding germanium dioxide into one of sodium carbonate(Na2CO3), calcium carbonate(CaCO3), potassium carbonate(K2CO3) or potassium bicarbonate(KHCO3) at the equivalent ratio, and sterilizing it. The obtained sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) is a germanium solution with high solubility and should be prepared, immediately prior to the use in the biological transformation process.
[56] In the 2nd Process of the present invention, in order for the yeast to be mutated, gamma rays (Co60) radiation of 2.0~2.5KGy (D10 value) for 2-4 hours into the protoplast fused yeast can be additionally made before culturing the yeast in the medium containing sodium metagerminate(Na2Ge03).
[57] Where the gamma rays (Co60) radiation is less than 2.0KGy, it may be difficult to obtain the desired mutated yeast because of too much colonies of the mutated strains formed on the plate. And, where gamma rays (Co60) radiation is more than 2.5KGy, it may be also difficult to obtain the desired mutated yeast because of too high extinction rate of the yeast.
[58] The mutated yeast where is irradiated by gamma rays (Co60) as mentioned above can be used for the subculture after culturing it in the YM broth for 1 hour and stabilizing the same.

[60] 3rd Process: Inflow of inorganic germanium ion into the yeast GP-Ol
[61] The yeast GP-Ol as obtained in the said 2nd process was cultivated with the solution of sodium metagermanate(Na2Ge03) for the yeast containing the high content of organic bio-germanium to be produced.

[63] The volume ratio of the added yeast and the solution of sodium metagermanate(Na2
GeO3) is 1:0.5-2.0.
[64] The obtained medium containing germanium was cultivated for 19-21 hours at pH
9-10 and 30~40C after adding the 0.01~0.04wt% of surfactant Thus, the yeast containing high content of organic bio-germanium(the yeast Ge-32K) of the present invention was produced.
[65] The added surfactant can lead to increasing the content of germanium in the cell by the increased permeability of the cell membrane. Thus, it is possible for the inorganic germanium to be inoculated into the mutated yeast, i.e., yeast GP-Ol(KCTC 11399BP).
[66] Preferably, the surfactant used in the present invention is Twin- 80, but is not limited to the above.
[67] Where the mixing ratio of the yeast and the solution of sodium metagermanate(Na2
GeO3) is less than 1:0.5 or over 1:2, it may lead to decreasing content of bio- germanium in the yeast due to the high viscosity of the suspension including the yeast and the lower diffusion speed between germanium and yeast cell walls.
[68] Further, where the value of pH is less than 9 or over 10, or the temperature is below
3O0C or over 4O0C, it may lead to decreasing the contents of bio-germanium in the yeast.
[69] In addition, where the surfactant is added in the culture medium containing germanium at less than 0.1wt%, the permeability of the cell wall is poor. Also, where the surfactant is added in the culture medium at over 0.4wt%, the permeability of membrane is inhibited as the structure of the yeast cell walls is broken. Thus, the said cases may result for the yeast to have the same contents of bio-germanium as the case where the surfactant is not added.
[70] After producing the yeast containing the high content of organic bio-germanium, i.e., the yeast Ge-32K, by using the mutated Saccharomyces cervisiae, i.e., yeast GP-Ol, the remaining medium containing the germanium can be re-used by filtering with 0.2/M membrane filter.
