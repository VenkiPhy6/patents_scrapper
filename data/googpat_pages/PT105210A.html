The mechanism of action of Metschnikowia pulcherrima on the inhibition of the pathogen is indicated as the competition for iron, an important element in the development of microorganisms, since when Metschnikowia pulcherrima and the pathogen are in a medium with little iron, Metschnikowia pulcherrima assimilates iron available to prevent the development of the pathogen. This inhibiting effect decreases as the iron concentration increases, that is to say, it is a limiting factor for the pathogen (Saravanakumar et al., Postharvest Biol. Tec 49: 121 (2008). The presence of Metschnikowia andauensis and the pathogen in medium with little iron does not cause inhibition of pathogen development, indicating that competition for this element is not the mechanism of responsible action of the ability antagonist of Metschnikowia andauensis, and this is one of the reasons why the antagonistic effect of the Metschnikowia andauensis species is unexpected. In relation to Metschnikowia fructicola, the Metschnikowia andauensis species is separated from this also on the basis of the analysis of the D1 / D2 subunit of the rDNA subunit, and in the physiological tests, by the production of the brown color pigment, known as pulcherrimin or pulcherim o, absent in the Metschnikowia fructicola species, which are characteristic enough for them to present different activities.
