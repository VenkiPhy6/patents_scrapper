52--5252fitness equilibriumDNA   Thanks to its nature, adaptive evolution always chooses the best way to accomplish the task (52). Random mutations across large populations and many generations allow evolution to try every possible solution to the problem. Only solutions that increase fitness-or at least not overtly harmful-survive to the next generation (52). This concept is particularly important given that adaptation to a niche environment is generally obtained at the expense of fitness outside that environment (52). As such, adaptive evolution allows an organism to reach the fitness equilibrium of its environment. This equilibrium consists of mutations that cause the desired adaptation and compensatory mutations that counteract as much as possible the overall loss of fitness. Thus, adaptively evolved mechanisms are often superior to targeted specific methods such as recombinant DNA engineering.
