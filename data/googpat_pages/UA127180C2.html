/ / . , , , .The present invention relates to the use of a probiotic composition for the treatment and/or prevention of psoriasis and/or psoriasis outbreaks. Thus, this invention can be covered by the field of medicine, in particular, in the treatment of skin diseases.

- , , , , , . , , , ' , . , . . , , , , , , .Psoriasis is an inflammatory and proliferative systemic disease characterized by the appearance of typical skin lesions in the form of multiple erythematous spots with well-defined edges covered with scaly lesions, mainly localized, but not exclusively, in the extensor areas of the limbs and scalp. So there is a hyperproliferative component affecting the epidermis associated with an inflammatory component affecting the dermis. The diagnosis of psoriasis is clinical, in most cases it does not require histological confirmation. The disease does not provide characteristic laboratory data. The pathogenesis of psoriasis is not very well known, and most authors believe that it is a genetically determined skin disease that is caused by various external factors, eventually causing lymphocyte infiltration and, ultimately, the typical skin lesions of the disease.

, (1 95) (3 95) , . - , , , .In general, psoriasis is thought to affect between one percent (1 95) and three percent (3 95) of the population in developed countries, although there is variability among European countries. In any case, the psychological, social and economic impact on the population is significant and comparable to other more well-known chronic disorders such as chronic bronchial disease, diabetes or depressive disorders.

, . , () 1-12 /-23. , - -1-23. , , , , , , , . , Because of the presumed immune origin of psoriasis, treatment for many years has been based on the use of toxic or systemic immunosuppressants to stop the immune component of the disease and thus relieve symptoms. This disease is characterized by an increase in inflammatory markers such as tumor necrosis factor (TNF) and interleukins 1-12 and I/-23. In recent years, significant progress has been made in its treatment with the development of immunomodulatory drugs such as anti-TME and monoclonal anti-1-23. These drugs are generally more effective than local or classical systemic treatments such as cyclosporine or corticosteroids, but in turn incur a high economic cost and can cause side effects in these patients, in particular, the onset of latent infectious diseases such as tuberculosis and viral hepatitis B, which leads to the indication and use of these drugs for some cases of severe psoriasis.
