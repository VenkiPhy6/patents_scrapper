So there is a hyperproliferative component affecting the epidermis associated with an inflammatory component affecting the dermis. The diagnosis of psoriasis is clinical, in most cases it does not require histological confirmation. The disease does not provide characteristic laboratory data. The pathogenesis of psoriasis is not very well known, and most authors believe that it is a genetically determined skin disease that is caused by various external factors, eventually causing lymphocyte infiltration and, ultimately, the typical skin lesions of the disease.

As shown in the examples appended to this specification, when administration of a probiotic composition (containing the microorganisms Viidobasietiit 5r. and I asiobasiiy5 5r.) together with conventional therapy to patients with psoriasis outbreaks, an additional thirty percent (30 95) of cases with complete improvement was observed more than in patients with psoriasis (or psoriatic patients) who received regular chronic treatment, but without the aforementioned probiotic composition.
