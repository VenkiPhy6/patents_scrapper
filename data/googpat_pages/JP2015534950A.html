  Herbivorous mites, in particular spider mites, are the main agricultural pests of orchards, greenhouses and many vegetable and fruit crops such as pepper, tomatoes, potatoes, corns, eggplants, cucumbers and strawberries. Tick uses its sharp mouth to damage leaves and / or fruit surfaces. In addition to direct damage to plant parts (referred to as spotting stippling), tick feeding also increases susceptibility to plant diseases.

  Mites are mites rather than insects, and few broad-spectrum insecticides are effective against mites. Due to the characteristics of ticks and the characteristics of available acaricides, the control of ticks is a challenge. For example, spider mites, the most economically important group of mites, are usually difficult to treat because they are usually on the backside of the leaves of plants. Furthermore, mites are known to become resistant within 2-4 years to currently available acaricides, many of which have a unimodal mode of action. There are few available acaricides that are active on mite eggs and need to be applied repeatedly. Therefore, there is a need for new acaricides that have strong residual activity that is translaminar and ovicidal in addition to good knockdown activity.
