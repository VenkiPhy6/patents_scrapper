
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 The claims defining the invention are as follows: 
   
 1. An isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia, wherein activity of phosphatidylethanolamine N-methyltransferase (PEMT) or phospholipid methyltransferase is inactivated. 
 
     
 2. The isolated strain of claim 1, wherein the phosphatidylethanolamine N methyltransferase is encoded by cho2 gene, and the cho2 gene consists of a polynucleotide sequence of SEQ ID NO: 1. 
 
     
 3. The isolated strain of claim 2, wherein the cho2 gene is deleted. 
 
     
 4. The isolated strain of claim 1, wherein the phospholipid methyltransferase is encoded by opi3 gene, and the opi3 gene consists of a polynucleotide sequence of SEQ ID NO: 11. 
 
     
 5. The isolated strain of claim 4, wherein the opi3 gene is deleted. 
 
     
 6. The isolated strain of claim 1, wherein the strain of the genus Yarrowia comprises Yarrowia lipolytica. 
 
     
 7. The isolated strain of claim 1, wherein the strain of the genus Yarrowia is a choline auxotrophic strain. 
 
     
 8. The isolated strain of claim 1, wherein the strain of the genus Yarrowia has an increased fat content, as compared with a wild-type. 
 
     
 9. The isolated strain of claim 8, wherein the fat comprises triacylglycerol (TAG). 
 
     
 10. A cosmetic composition comprising one or more of the isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia of any one of claims 1 to 9, a culture of the strain, an extract of the strain, a dry product of the strain, a lysate of the strain, and a fat recovered from one or more of the strain, the culture, the extract, the dry product, and the lysate. 
 
     
 11. A food composition comprising one or more of the isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia of any one of claims 1 to 9, a culture of the strain, an extract of the strain, a dry product of the strain, a lysate of the strain, and a fat recovered from one or more of the strain, the culture, the extract, the dry product, and the lysate. 
 
     
 12. A feed composition comprising one or more of the isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia of any one of claims 1 to 9, a culture of the strain, an extract of the strain, a dry product of the strain, a lysate of the strain, and a fat recovered from one or more of the strain, the culture, the extract, the dry product, and the lysate. 
 
     
 13. A medical composition comprising one or more of the isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia of any one of claims 1 to 9, a culture of the strain, an extract of the strain, a dry product of the strain, a lysate of the strain, and a fat recovered from one or more of the strain, the culture, the extract, the dry product, and the lysate. 
 
     
 14. A method of increasing a fat in a strain, comprising: culturing an isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia, wherein activity of phosphatidylethanolamine N methyltransferase (PEMT) or phospholipid methyltransferase is inactivated. 
 
     
 15. The method of claim 14, wherein the culturing the strain is culturing the strain in a medium containing choline. 
 
     
 16. The method of claim 15, wherein a concentration of the choline in the medium is from 0.05 mM to 5 mM. 
 
     
 17. The method of claim 15, wherein a C/N ratio in the medium is from 40 to 80. 
 
     
 18. The method of claim 14, wherein the fat comprises triacylglycerol (TAG). 
 
     
 19. A method of preparing a fat, comprising: culturing an isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia, wherein activity of phosphatidylethanolamine N-methyltransferase (PEMT) or phospholipid methyltransferase is inactivated. 
 
     
 20. The method of claim 19, further comprising: recovering the fat from the strain, a culture thereof, an extract thereof, a dry product thereof, or alysate thereof, after the culturing the strain. 
 
     
 21. The method of claim 19, wherein the culturing the strain is culturing the strain in a medium containing choline. 
 
     
 22. The method of claim 21, wherein a C/N ratio in the medium is from 40 to 80. 
 
     
 23. Use of an isolated strain of the genus Yarrowia, wherein activity of phosphatidylethanolamine N-methyltransferase (PEMT) or phospholipid methyltransferase is inactivated; a culture of the strain; an extract of the strain; a dry product of the strain; a lysate of the strain; a cosmetic composition, a food composition, a feed composition, or a medical composition, each comprising one or more of the strain, the culture, the extract, the dry product, and the lysate, and a fat recovered from one or more of the strain, the culture, the extract, the dry product, and the lysate in fat production.
