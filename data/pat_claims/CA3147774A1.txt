
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  THE CLAIMS DEFINING THE INVENTION ARE AS FOLLOWS:  
   
 1.    A method for profiling endophyte strains from a microbiome, said method including the steps of: providing a microbiome; obtaining protein profile spectra from one or more endophytes of the microbiome; processing the protein profile spectra; clustering the endophyte strains based on the processed protein profile spectra; and selecting and/or isolating endophyte strain(s) having desired genetic and/or metabolic characteristics, or being phylogenetically related to a desired endophyte strain.  
 
     
 2.    A method according to claim 1, wherein the microbiome is isolated from a plant material.  
 
     
 3.    A method according to claim 1 or 2, wherein the plant material is from a plant selected from perennial ryegrass (Lolium perenne), tall fescue (Festuca arundinaceae), corn (Zea mays), Glycine species, wheat (Triticum aestivum) and barley (Hordeum vulgare).  
 
     
 4.    A method according to claim 3, wherein the Glycine species is selected from the group consisting of Glycine tomentella, Glycine tabacina, Glycine latifolia, Glycine hirticaulis, Glycine microphylla, Glycine clandestine.  
 
     
 5.    A method according to claim 3, wherein the Glycine species is Glycine max.  
 
     
 6.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 5, wherein the plant material includes seeds, leaves, stems, petioles, roots, buds, flowers or any combination thereof.  
 
     
 7.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 6, wherein the microbiome includes bacteria and/or fungi.  
 
     
 8.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 7, wherein the step of providing the microbiome includes the steps of: providing plant material; washing the plant material in an aqueous solution;     submerging the plant material in a aqueous solution; macerating the plant material; and applying the macerated plant material to a growth medium for growth of the microbiota to provide the isolated microbiome.  
 
     
 9.    A method according to claim 8, wherein the microbiota grown on the growth medium are subjected to a re-streaking so as to obtain an isolated endophyte colony.  
 
     
 10.    A method according to claim 8 or 9, wherein the plant material includes a seed, and wherein the step of providing the microbiome includes the preliminary step of: harvesting the plant material; sterilising the plant material; germinating the plant material; and growing the germinated plant material.  
 
     
 11.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 10, wherein the protein profile spectra are obtained by mass spectrometry.  
 
     
 12.    A method according to claim 11, wherein the mass spectrometry includes matrix assisted laser desorption/ionisation (MALDI) mass spectrometry.  
 
     
 13.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 12, wherein the protein profile spectra are processed by a data deconvolution workflow.  
 
     
 14.    A method according to claim 13, wherein the data deconvolution workflow includes performing the steps of: a m/z scan to create a m/z grid; a spectrum baseline subtraction; and a m/z alignment; wherein the data deconvolution workflow provides the processed protein spectra.  
 
     
 15.    A method according to claim 14, wherein the m/z grid is produced according to an adaptive grid method.  
 
     
 16.    A method according to claim 14 or 15, wherein the spectrum baseline subtraction is performed according to a quantile normalization method.  
 
     
 17.    A method according to any one of claims 14 to 16, wherein the m/z alignment is performed with reference to a reference spectrum.  
 
     
 18.    A method according to claim 17, wherein the reference spectrum is of Escherichia coli ATCC 25922.  
 
     
 19.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 18, wherein protein profile spectra are obtained from one or more isolated endophyte colonies and wherein processing the protein profiles includes combining the protein profile spectra of each isolated endophyte colony.  
 
     
 20.    A method according to claim 19 further including subsequently performing the steps of: m/z alignment; spectrum smoothing; m/z range restriction; spectrum peak detection; and valid peak filtration to removing peaks which do not meet a defined threshold.  
 
     
 21.    A method according to claim 20, wherein the processed protein profiles are converted into a matrix for analysis.  
 
     
 22.    A method according to claim 20 or 21, wherein the m/z ratio alignment is performed with reference to a reference spectrum.  
 
     
 23.    A method according to claim 22, wherein the reference spectrum is of Escherichia coli ATCC 25922.  
 
     
 24.    A method according to any one of claims 20 to 23, wherein the spectrum smoothing is performed according to a moving average algorithm.  
 
     
 25.    A method according to any one of claims 20 to 24, wherein the m/z range is restricted to between 2000 Da and 20000 Da.  
 
     
 26.    A method according to any one of claims 20 to 25, wherein the spectrum peak detection is performed by a resolution-based method.  
 
     
 27.    A method according to any one of claims 20 to 26, wherein the valid feature filter has a threshold between approximately 0-40% intensity.  
 
     
 28.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 27, wherein processed protein profile spectra are used to perform hierarchal clustering.  
 
     
 29.    A method according to claim 28, wherein said hierarchical clustering provides a clade of endophytes having properties selected from: i.          related bioactivity; related geographic ranges; or belonging to plant lines having the same phenotype.  
 
     
 30.    A method according to claim 28, wherein said hierarchical clustering provides a clade of endophytes having properties including similar protein profiles.  
 
     
 31.    A method according to claim 29 or 30, wherein the related bioactivity is selected from bioprotection and biofertilizer activity.  
 
     
 32.    A method according to claim 29 or 30, wherein the plant phenotype is for drought tolerance or drought resistance.  
 
     
 33.    A method according to any one of claims 1 to 32, wherein prior to profiling endophyte strains from a microbiome, plant microbiome profiling is performed, said plant microbiome profiling including the steps of: providing plant material from a first plant species and plant material from a related second plant species; characterising the microbiome of the first and second plant species by analysing the plant material; and assessing the microbiome of the first and second plant species to identify endophyte strains found in both the first and second plant species, or endophyte strains found in the second plant species but not in the first plant species.  
 
     
 34.    A method for profiling a plant microbiome said method including the steps of:  providing plant material from a first plant species and material from a related second plant species; characterising the microbiome of the first and second plant species by analysing the plant material; and assessing the microbiome of the first and second plant species to identify endophyte strains found in both the first and second plant species or endophyte strains found in the second plant species but not in the first plant species.  
 
     
 35.    A method according to claim 33 or 34, wherein the first plant species is selected from perennial ryegrass (Lolium perenne), tall fescue (Festuca arundinaceae), corn (Zea mays), Glycine species, wheat (Triticum aestivum) and barley (Hordeum vulgare).  
 
     
 36.    A method according to claim 35, wherein the first plant species is selected from Glycine tomentella, Glycine tabacina, Glycine latifolia, Glycine hirticaulis, Glycine microphylla, Glycine clandestine and Glycine Max.  
 
     
 37.    A method according to any one of claims 33 to 36, wherein the second plant species is a crop wild relative (CRVV) of the first plant species.  
 
     
 38.    A method according to any one of claims 33 to 37, wherein providing the plant material from the first and second plant species includes the steps of: sterilising the plant material; and germinating the plant material, wherein the germinated plant material provides seedlings for characterisation.  
 
     
 39.    A method according to any one of claims 33 to 38, wherein the plant material is selected from seeds, leaves, stems, petioles, roots, buds, flowers or any combination thereof.  
 
     
 40.    A method according to any one of claims 33 to 39, wherein characterising the microbiome of the first and second plant species includes the steps of: extracting nucleic acid from the plant material; and analysing the extracted nucleic acid to profile the plant material microbiome.  
 
     
 41.    A method according to any one of claims 33 to 40, wherein assessing the microbiome of the first and second plant species includes statistical analysis to determine microbiome operational taxomic units (OTU) present within each plant species.  
 
     
 42.    A method according to claim 41, wherein the microbiome OTU within the first plant species are compared to that of the second plant species to provides a means for determining microbiota which are either: shared between the plant species, or unique to the second plant species, relative to the first plant species.  
 
     
 43.    A method according claim 41 or 42, wherein the identified microbiome OTU are selected from the group including Stenotrophomonas sp., Pseudomonas sp., Acinetobacter sp., Holomonas sp., Enterobactereaceae sp., Pantoea sp., Burkholderiaceae sp., Ralstonia sp., Massilia sp., Herbaspirillum sp., Delftia sp., Curvibacter sp., Aquabacterium sp., Sphingomonas sp., Novosphingobium sp., Bradyrhizobium sp., Ochrobactrum sp.,Methylobacterium sp., Lactobacillus sp., Staphylococcus sp., Bacillus sp.  and Curtobacterium sp.

A method of enhancing the bioactivity of a plant species, said method including:  identifying endophyte strains found in the first and second plant species according to the method of any one of claims 33 to 43; and transferring one or more endophyte strains from the second plant species to the first plant species to enhance bioactivity of the first plant species; wherein the transferred endophyte enhances bioactivity of the first plant species.
