
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
 
  THE CLAIMS DEFINING THE INVENTION ARE AS FOLLOWS:  
   
 1.    A substantially purified or isolated Pseudomonas sp. endophyte of a plant of the Cannabaceae fam ily.  
 
     
 2.    An endophyte according to claim 1, wherein said endophyte is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype to the plant or part thereof from which it is substantially purified or isolated and/or is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype to a plant or part thereof to which it is inoculated.  
 
     
 3.    An endophyte according to claim 2, wherein the bioprotection phenotype is improved resistance to pests and/or diseases.  
 
     
 4.    An endophyte according to claim 3, wherein said disease is infection by a fungal pathogen.  
 
     
 5.    An endophyte according to claim 4, wherein said fungal pathogen is a Botrytis sp.  fungus or a Fusarium sp. fungus.  
 
     
 6.      An endophyte according to claim 4 or 5, wherein said fungal pathogen is a Botrytis cinerea or a Fusarium proliferatum species strain.  
 
     
 7.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 6, wherein said endophyte is capable of presenting with an in vitro resistance to a pathogenic Botrytis cinerea species strain of at least about 75% against no-endophyte control, and/or of at least about 20%  greater resistance than a Paenibacillus pabuli species bacterial strain.  
 
     
 8.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 7, wherein said endophyte is capable of presenting with an in vitro resistance to a pathogenic Fusarium proliferatum species strain of at least about 45% against no-endophyte control, and/or of at least about 30% greater resistance than a Paenibacillus pabuli species bacterial strain.  
 
     
 9.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 8, wherein said endophyte is positive for one or more of the fragin, arylpolyene (APE), bacteriocin, NAGGN, betalactone, cupriachelin, non-ribosomal peptide (NRP) siderophore, T1PKS (e.g. entolysin), siderophor, hserlactone, pyoverdin, crochelin and NRP + polyketide biosynthesis gene clusters.  
 
     
 10.               An endophyte according to claim 9, wherein said endophyte is positive for one or more of the APE Vf (arylpolyene), fengycin (betalactone) and syringomycin (hserlactone) biosynthesis gene clusters.  
 
     
 11.    An endophyte according to claim 10, wherein said endophyte is positive for the syringomycin biosynthesis gene cluster.  
 
     
 12.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 11, wherein said endophyte is negative for one or more secondary metabolite biosynthesis gene clusters commonly associated with human toxicity.  
 
     
 13.    An endophyte according to claim 12, wherein a secondary metabolite biosynthesis gene cluster commonly associated with human toxicity and for which the endophyte is negative is the pyocyanin gene cluster.  
 
     
 14.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 2 to 13, wherein said plant or part thereof to which it is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype is one which is or may be infected by a Botrytis sp. fungus and/or a Fusarium sp. fungus.  
 
     
 15.    An endophyte according to claim 14, wherein said plant or part thereof to which it is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype is of the Vitis, Cannabaceae, Fragaria, Rubus, Vaccinium, Ribes, Solanum, Brassica, Phaseolus and/or Lactuca families.  
 
     
 16.    An endophyte according to claim 15, wherein said plant or part thereof to which it is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype is of the Vitis and/or Cannabaceae family.  
 
     
 17.    An endophyte according to claim 16, wherein said plant or part thereof to which it is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype is a V vinifera species plant and/or a C.  sativa species plant.  
 
     
 18.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 17, wherein said endophyte is capable of conferring in planta resistance to a plant of the Vitis vinifera species to a pathogenic Botrytis cinerea species strain of a pathogen infection score at most about 2.2, against no-endophyte control.  
 
     
 19.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 18, wherein said plant of the Cannabaceae family is a Cannabis sativa, Cannabis indica or Cannabis ruderalis species plant.  
 
     
 20.               An endophyte according to claim 19, wherein said plant of the Cannabaceae farnily is a C. sativa species plant.  
 
     
 21.    An endophyte according to claim 20, wherein said C. sativa species plant is a line denoted CannBio 2, CannBio 3, CannBio 4 and/or CannBio 5.  
 
     
 22.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 21, wherein said endophyte is substantially purified or isolated from a flower, flower bract, leaf, petiole, stern or root of the plant of the Cannabaceae family.  
 
     
 23.    An endophyte according to claim 22, wherein said endophytes is substantially purified or isolated frorn a root.  
 
     
 24.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 23, wherein said endophyte is a Pseudomonas corrugata species strain.  
 
     
 25.    An endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 24, wherein said endophyte is a strain denoted EB-010, EB-013, EB-017 and/or EB-117 as deposited with The National Measurement Institute on 24 November 2020 with accession numbers V20/025722, V20/025723, V20/025725 and V20/025727, respectively.  
 
     
 26.    A substantially purified or isolated bioprotective endophyte selected from the group consisting of EB-010, EB-013, EB-017 and EB-117 as deposited with The National Measurement Institute on 24 November 2020 with accession numbers V20/025722, V20/025723, V20/025725 and V20/025727, respectively.  
 
     
 27.    A plant or part thereof inoculated with one or more endophytes according to any one of claims 1 to 26.  
 
     
 28.    A plant, plant propagative material or other plant part derived from a plant according to claim 27 and infected with said one or more of said endophytes.  
 
     
 29.    A plant or part thereof inoculated with one or more endophytes according to any one of claims 1 to 26 and exhibiting a bioprotection phenotype conferred to the plant by the endophyte.  
 
     
 30.    Use of an endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 26 to produce a plant or part thereof infected with said one or more endophytes.  
 
     
 31.                                                                                   A method for conferring a bioprotection phenotype to a plant or part thereof, said method including inoculating the plant or part thereof with an endophyte according to any one of claims 1 to 26.  
 
     
 32.                                                                                   A method for selecting a bioprotective endophyte of a plant of the Cannabaceae family, said method comprising: a. substantially purifying or isolating one or more endophytes; and b. subjecting said one or more endophytes to one or more of:      i. an in vitro bioprotection activity assay; ii. an in planta bioprotection activity assay; and iii. an analysis for a secondary metabolite biosynthesis gene cluster associated with the production of biocidal compounds, and based thereon, selecting an endophyte which is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype to the plant from which it is substantially purified or isolated and/or is capable of conferring a bioprotection phenotype to a plant or part thereof to which it is inoculated.  
 
     
 33.                                                                                   A method according to claim 32, further including subjecting said endophyte colonies or selected endophyte(s) to genetic analysis to identify the endophyte species.  
 
     
 34.                                                                                   A method according to claim 33, wherein a selected endophyte is a Pseudomonas sp. endophyte.  
 
     
 35.                                                                        A  method according to any one of claims 32 to 34, wherein a selected endophyte is substantially purified or isolated from the roots a plant of the Cannabaceae family.
